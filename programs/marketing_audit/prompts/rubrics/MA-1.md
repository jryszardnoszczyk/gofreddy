# MA-1 — Strategic Narrative Coherence

**Status:** DRAFT (master plan §6.4 working title; JR review-iterate before manifest freeze)

## What this rubric scores

The deliverable's `findings.md` is organized around **one strategic argument** per section, not a list of issues. Every finding within a section serves the section's thesis, and every section serves a unifying audit-level thesis surfaced in the State-of-the-Business opener.

This is one of the two hardest rubrics. Unifying findings under a strategic story is the difference between an audit that changes how a Head of Marketing thinks and one that's a checklist of problems they already half-knew about.

## What "good" looks like

- Each section opens with a 1-2 sentence thesis statement
- Findings within the section build the thesis (causal chain, additive evidence, or contrasting facets)
- The State-of-the-Business opener names the audit's central argument
- Cross-section findings reinforce each other; contradictions are surfaced and resolved
- A reader who only read the section thesis lines could explain the audit's argument

## What "bad" looks like

- Section opens with a list-of-findings format ("We found that...")
- Findings within a section are unrelated tactical observations
- No through-line connecting sections
- Findings contradict each other without acknowledgment
- Reader walks away with N disconnected problems, not a strategic frame

## Score scale (0-10)

- **0-2** Sections are bullet-list of findings; no thesis structure; reads as "checklist of issues"
- **3-4** Some sections have implicit thesis; most read as findings-list
- **5-6** Most sections have explicit thesis; through-line is weak across sections
- **7-8** Every section has explicit thesis; through-line is present and supported
- **9-10** Audit-level central argument is named in State-of-the-Business and every section advances it

## Anchors for severity-of-MA-1-failure

- A section with 5+ findings and zero thesis: severity-of-failure 3
- An audit-level State-of-the-Business that names the argument but sections don't follow: severity-of-failure 2
- One section that lists tactical issues while others have thesis: severity-of-failure 1
