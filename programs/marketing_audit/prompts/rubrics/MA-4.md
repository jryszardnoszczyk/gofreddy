# MA-4 — Actionable + Capability-Mapped

**Status:** DRAFT (master plan §6.4; JR review-iterate before manifest freeze)

## What this rubric scores

Each ParentFinding's `recommendation` is **strategic** (≥50 words of substance, names what would solve this in terms the agency engagement delivers) AND maps cleanly to a `capability_registry` tier (`fix_it` / `build_it` / `run_it`). Recommendations are NOT DIY execution guides; they describe the work the prospect would commission.

## What "good" looks like

- Every recommendation ≥50 words of strategic substance
- Recommendation names the engagement scope (e.g. "rebuild positioning + 90d cross-channel rollout")
- Recommendation maps to a capability_registry entry with the right tier
- Cost-of-delay framing where it fits ("each month without X, the prospect loses ~$Y")
- The proposal in `proposal.md` references finding IDs for every tier entry

## What "bad" looks like

- Recommendations are bullet lists of tactical steps ("do X, then Y, then Z")
- Recommendations <50 words OR generic ("invest more in content marketing")
- Recommendations describe DIY execution ("write 3 case studies per quarter")
- No capability_registry mapping; recommendations float untied
- Proposal tier entries don't trace back to findings

## Score scale (0-10)

- **0-2** Recommendations are tactical bullet lists; no capability mapping
- **3-4** Some recommendations strategic; many DIY-flavored; sparse capability mapping
- **5-6** Most recommendations strategic ≥50 words; partial capability mapping
- **7-8** All recommendations strategic ≥50 words + tier-mapped; proposal references findings
- **9-10** Above + cost-of-delay framing on high-severity findings; capability mapping is internally consistent across the proposal

## Anchors for severity-of-MA-4-failure

- A recommendation that's a DIY execution guide: severity-of-failure 3
- A recommendation <50 words on a high-severity (3) finding: severity-of-failure 2
- A finding with no proposal_tier_mapping or capability link: severity-of-failure 2
