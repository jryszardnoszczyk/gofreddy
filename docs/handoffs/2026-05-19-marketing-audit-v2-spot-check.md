# Marketing Audit v2 spot-check

## §1 Reader spec verdict

**ALIGNED, with a residual taste concern that matches CI v3.5.** v2 correctly broadened from "B2B SaaS only" to six US-primary verticals (SaaS, AI lab, agency, service firm, finance, e-commerce) and explicitly demoted Polish first-cohort (Klinika, DWF) to "concrete fixtures, NOT architectural target." That correction is the right answer to JR's first-cohort-overfitting concern.

Residual concerns. (a) **The "commissioning context spans three shapes" framing is the rejected A/B/C routing relabeled.** v2 explicitly disowns Cluster A (personnel) / Cluster B (operational) / Cluster C (strategic) — and then immediately reintroduces the same three buckets as "personnel context / operational context / strategic context" that "shape the prescription's emphasis inside the same comprehensive audit." Look at the §1 prose carefully: each of the three contexts has a specific mapped emphasis (personnel → operator-decision attribution + run_it interim-coverage; operational → 30/60/90 + budget; strategic → Axis 1 + build_it dollar weight). MA-4's score-1 anchor *enforces* that the audit's "detail level matches the commissioning context's emphasis." This is cluster routing implemented as a per-fixture context flag rather than a per-fixture artifact-shape flag. JR rejected the artifact-level routing; the spec evaded the rejection by moving routing to the criterion-prose-and-emphasis layer. **The same Goodhart attack surface as the v1 cluster routing now lives inside MA-4** — workflow learns to slot-fill the "personnel emphasis" markers when it detects a personnel fixture. Either kill the three-context framing entirely and let the comprehensive audit be invariant, or surface this as a known compromise JR signed off on. As written, the spec is straddling.

(b) **The reader description is doing too much rhetorical work.** "Smart, time-poor, runway-aware, and skeptical. They have consumed enough consultant-grade decks…" is the same load-bearingless phrasing CI v3.5 carried and the same criticism applies: every founder reading any 2026 audit fits this. The phrase that actually discriminates is "will quote one or two sentences from the audit if challenged later" — that's the falsifiable test. The "skeptical" framing is decorative.

(c) **Six verticals + Polish overlay + sibling-fork triggers is a substantial complexity load** the spec acknowledges in §8 question 3 ("Need 6–8 additional fixtures before locking the comprehensive scope via redundancy check") but does NOT gate ship on. The spec is locking criteria against a substrate that hasn't been validated for the verticals the criteria explicitly serve.

## §1.5 Artifact shape verdict

**MISALIGNED — this is the load-bearing concern of the spot-check, and it matches the CI v3.5 modular-package failure mode.** The multi-file 5–7 deliverable (findings 5–9k + roadmap 1.5–3k + proposal 1–2k + cuts_reduces_adds 1.5–2.5k + gap_report 0.5–1.5k + optional vertical/geo overlays) is research-derived without client validation. Four concrete concerns:

1. **The substrate-already-supports-it claim is materially false.** v2 §1.5 (d) asserts: "v006 has `findings.md`, `proposal.md`, `gap_report.md` wired. Adding `roadmap.md`, `cuts_reduces_adds.md`, optional vertical / geo overlays is incremental." I checked the actual session output in `/Users/jryszardnoszczyk/Documents/GitHub/gofreddy/autoresearch/archive/v006/sessions/marketing_audit/`. Out of 4 real client sessions (DWF, Anthropic, Perplexity, Stripe): `findings.md` exists in 4/4 ✓, `gap_report.md` in 3/4, `proposal.md` in **1/4** (Stripe only), `roadmap.md` in **0/4**, `cuts_reduces_adds.md` in **0/4**, vertical/geo overlays in **0/4**. The MA workflow currently produces ONE file reliably. The "multi-file deliverable" the spec locks the judge against is, in production, a single-file deliverable today. The spec describes the artifact gofreddy wants the lane to ship, not the artifact the lane actually ships. Locking a 5–7-file multi-file judge surface against a 1-file substrate creates a structural_gate that fails 100% of current sessions on Day 1. Either the workflow ships first and the spec follows, or the spec gates on workflow-side multi-file emission landing. The current sequencing has the judge spec leading the substrate by a full implementation cycle and the spec doesn't acknowledge it.

2. **The word-count band is calibrated against an aspirational ceiling, not real output.** Real findings.md word counts in production sessions are 1042 (Anthropic), 1868 (Perplexity), 2639 (DWF), 6156 (Stripe) — median ~2,200 words. The v2 spec's band is 5,000–9,000 words for findings.md alone. The current live structural_gate caps findings.md at 8,000 words ("max 8000 — over-length kills MA-6 polish") — v2 extends the ceiling to 9,000 without justification beyond "Kalungi's 95-point audit sits in this range." Three of four production sessions are sub-3k. Locking the band at 5–9k means three of four current fixtures are structural_gate failures. This is the same shape-bifurcation risk CI v3.5 carried: the spec describes the deliverable the lane WANTS to produce; the workflow produces something materially shorter; the evolution loop now has 50 generations to climb a 2× word-count cliff while also being penalized by per-file word-count band ceilings if it overshoots, AND simultaneously being scored against new files it doesn't emit. The token-cost envelope question (§8 q4) acknowledges the cost ramp ($300–500/audit, possibly $500 hard breaker) but not the substrate readiness gap.

3. **Multi-file as reader-routing without cluster-routing is the same pattern relabeled.** v2 §1.5 (a) defends multi-file: "founder reads findings.md for the strategic narrative; head-of-growth reads cuts_reduces_adds.md for the defensible recommendations; operations lead reads roadmap.md for the execution plan; procurement reads proposal.md for the engagement scope." This IS reader-routing at the artifact level — exactly what was supposedly rejected. The spec defends a multi-file design where DIFFERENT FILES serve DIFFERENT READERS. CI v3.5's modular-package architecture was flagged for the same reason; the same reasoning applies here. The "single source of truth diagnostic" framing is the rhetorical hedge; in practice the lane produces 5–7 files because different readers read different ones. There is no evidence cited that any real MA client (Anthropic, Perplexity, DWF, Klinika) has consumed a 5–7-file deliverable and that the file-split mapped onto how they actually read it. The exemplars cited (Kalungi, Bell Curve, ProfitWell, Wynter, Animalz) all ship ONE deliverable to the client, not five. None of them is a published 5-file split.

4. **9-section deliverable shell coexisting with 12 macro-axes is genuinely inconsistent in a way the spec hand-waves.** v2 §8 fold F8 declares "Both structures coexist: the 9-section shell organizes the deliverable surface area the agency engagement covers; the 12-axis structure organizes the reader-facing strategic argument. Live code's STRUCTURAL_DOC_FACTS tuple already encodes the 9-section check; preserve." But §4 MA-1's score-1 anchor scopes the diagnostic *as 12-axis*; the workflow's `REQUIRED_SECTIONS` enforces 9 named sections (findability / narrative / acquisition / experience / competitive / monitoring / geo / state_of_business / martech_compliance); these are NOT the same 12 axes (Axis 1 Strategic Foundations does not map to one of the 9 sections cleanly — it spans `narrative`, `state_of_business`, and part of `competitive`). The structural_gate will check for 9 sections; the judge will look for 12 axes; the workflow has to satisfy both simultaneously. Either the workflow runs two organizing schemas in parallel (forcing each finding to be labeled twice), or the workflow drops one and the spec's other half misfires. The spec's "they coexist" handwave does not specify HOW the workflow synthesizes 12 reader-shaped axes FROM 9 engineering-shaped lens-buckets without producing a deliverable that internally repeats itself. The honest move would be to pick one and retire the other; the spec preserves both as F8.

Recommended posture: ship findings.md only as production-default with the 12-axis structure replacing the 9-section shell entirely, treat the other 4–6 files as deliverables the workflow CAN produce on demand but does not gate the judge on, and lock the spec only after a real fixture has actually emitted at least 3 of the 5 required files end-to-end at agency-engagement quality.

## §4 Criteria spot-check (per criterion)

### MA-1 — Comprehensive diagnostic synthesizes into strategic narrative + binding constraint

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD with drift.** The "ONE strategic argument" + "2 independent named evidence sources" + "sea of 3's defeats the diagnostic discipline" load-bearing elements all carry. The three vertical examples (SaaS $2.4M / DTC $5M/mo / agency $3M) discriminate. **However:** the anchor now folds in "12-axis comprehensive diagnostic synthesizes into a strategic narrative" — which is a comprehensiveness check the design guide explicitly warns against ("Do not score: number of axes covered" appears as a "Do not score" line). The criterion now both rewards synthesis-into-one-narrative AND structurally pushes against axis-coverage gaming. Workflow under selection pressure will read "synthesize 12 axes into one argument" as "name all 12 axes in the opening paragraph for synthesis credit." The Phase 4 rollback pathology is reachable from this anchor without modification.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** "Sea of 3's," "single-source extrapolation presented as multi-source," confabulated metric — names specific failure modes.
- 4-step CoT: **GOOD.** Step 2 evidence-traceability + Step 3 severity discipline are load-bearing. **Concern on Step 3:** the judge cannot verify "ParentFinding severity = max of children (rollup rule)" without the SubSignal substrate visible to it; this is a structural_gate check misrouted to the judge. Either pass SubSignal severities into the judge's input or route the rollup-rule check to structural_gate.

**Severity calibration "not a sea of 3's" — sufficient?** Mostly. The phrasing is concrete enough to discriminate at the score-1 anchor. **But:** the spec does NOT name a quantitative threshold (what fraction of 3's triggers a score-0?). The Goodhart-resistant version would be "if >60% of SubSignals have severity=3, score 0 on MA-1." Without a threshold, the judge will hallucinate the cut-line. This is consistent with the guide's no-numeric-bias-clauses posture, but the calibration discipline is then truly load-bearing on lens-specific `severity_anchors` in the rubric YAML — which the spec asserts exists ("anchored to lens-specific `severity_anchors`") but does not reference a path to or verify is current. If the per-lens anchors drift or aren't maintained, MA-1 collapses to subjective severity discipline.

### MA-2 — Every CUT / REDUCE / ADD maps to capability_registry tier with action + owner + budget + metric

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD, with a structural concern.** Six required elements (action + tier + owner + budget + timeline + metric + kill-trigger) is a lot to enforce per recommendation; with 3–7 CUTS + 3–6 REDUCES + 3–6 ADDS, that's 9–19 recommendations × 7 elements = 63–133 checks. The CoT Step 2 "verify presence of all 7 elements for each" is a heavy lift for a single-shot judge on a 5,000–9,000 word file. Real risk: judge fatigue → late-list recommendations get less scrutiny → workflow learns to put the strongest recommendations at the top of the CUTS / REDUCES / ADDS lists and lower-quality ones in the tail. The list-position-bias documented in arxiv 2410.02736 (style bias 0.76-0.92, position bias ≤0.04 on frontier) is small in absolute terms but compounds when judge attention is rationed across 19 sub-items.
- **Modern-lever bias load-bearing test.** "12 canonical mediocre cuts as ITS OWN ADDS = score 0" is the right discriminator — strong, specific, falsifiable. The 3 vertical examples (SaaS founder-led + DTC SMS + service-firm partner-content) all bias toward modern levers. **However:** the list of 12 mediocre cuts overlaps with itself: #2 broetry-LinkedIn and #5 broetry-LinkedIn-carryover are the same item; the spec acknowledges this ("see #2") but the duplicate damages the literal count. Workflow under selection pressure will read 12 = 11; if it grep-matches against 12 and one is a duplicate, the dedupe logic is non-obvious. Either drop #5 or rename it. Currently the literal "12 phrases" check exists in MA_BANNED_PHRASES (verified — 12 distinct strings in live code), so the deliverable-substrate count is OK; the criterion-prose canonical-cut list is what needs the fix.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.**
- CoT: **GOOD.** Step 3 "Verify NONE of the 12 canonical mediocre cuts" is the load-bearing modern-lever-bias check.

### MA-3 — Every recommendation traces to a revenue mechanism

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD with vertical drift concern.** The 7-vertical metric list (B2B SaaS / AI lab / agency / service firm / finance / DTC / local-services) is comprehensive but combinatorial — the judge has to first classify the fixture's vertical, then check that the cited metrics belong to the vertical's allowed list. Vertical classification is itself a judgment call (gofreddy itself is "agency" but also "service firm," a derm clinic is "local-services" but also "healthcare," Anthropic is "AI lab" but also "B2B SaaS"). Without a deterministic vertical-classifier in the workflow input passed to the judge, MA-3's vertical-appropriateness check is double-imagination (judge imagines the vertical, then imagines the appropriate metrics). The CI-4 "imagine the prior" pathology applies here with a different name.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** "Trial-to-paid for no-trial brand; AUM growth for early-stage SaaS" are concrete enough to discriminate.
- Three vertical examples: **GOOD.** "CFO line" framing is load-bearing and falsifiable.

### MA-4 — Stage diagnostic + commissioning-context emphasis match + Phase-0 integration + refusal

This is the criterion that worries me most. **DRIFT — too many independent dimensions folded into one criterion.** MA-4 now requires the judge to verify FOUR distinct things in one outcome question:
1. Stage named with at least one observable vertical-appropriate anchor.
2. At least one recommendation explicitly refused or sequenced on stage grounds.
3. Phase-0 measurements integrated into diagnostic (state-of-business pulls from phase0_meta.json; per-section coloring; nulls surface as findings).
4. Detail level matches commissioning context (personnel / operational / strategic emphasis).

The first three are coherent; they all test "audit names where the company actually sits and refuses to pretend otherwise." Element 4 — commissioning-context emphasis match — is a different epistemological move. The judge has to (a) infer which commissioning context applies (personnel vs operational vs strategic) — itself unstated in most fixtures, (b) verify the audit's detail emphasis matches the inferred context. This is the same "imagine the unstated prior" pathology CI-4 carried that the v3.5 review flagged. The fixture does not state "this was commissioned for personnel reasons"; the judge will hallucinate the commissioning context and then check the audit against the hallucination. Variance per generation will be high specifically on element 4. Recommend: drop element 4 from MA-4 entirely and let the comprehensive scope be invariant of commissioning context, OR move element 4 to the structural_gate as "if `engagement_context` flag is present in fixture metadata, verify audit emphasizes the matching dimensions" (deterministic) and out of the judge entirely.

Concern (b). **Element 3 (Phase-0 integration) is potentially a feature-check.** "State-of-business opener pulls from `phase0_meta.json`" is observable as: does the opening section mention the 9 named meta-frames? Workflow learns to slot-fill the 9 names into the opener. The score-1 anchor's phrasing "per-section findings color by relevant Phase-0 frames where applicable" is just vague enough that the workflow could ship a sentence per section name-checking the corresponding Phase-0 measurement and pass the criterion. The deeper test would be: does the audit's recommendation set change when Phase-0 measurements change? But that's only measurable across fixtures, not within one. As written, MA-4 element 3 is at risk of being the Phase 4 feature-checking pathology recurring under a different name. Strongest defense: phrase the anchor as "the audit's recommendations would be materially different if any of the 9 Phase-0 measurements were inverted." That tests dependence, not surface mention.

### MA-5 — Upstream constraint named, sequenced not parallel + positioning as 6th class

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD with strong concern.** "Sequenced behind the upstream fix" + "positioning as load-bearing 6th upstream class" are the load-bearing distinctions; the three vertical examples (SaaS retention + DTC product-fit + service-firm positioning) actually demonstrate sequencing rather than parallel-tracking. The score-0 anchor enumerates eight failure modes — heaviest score-0 list of any criterion, which is appropriate given MA-5's primary role.
- **Positioning as 6th upstream class is the right call.** Positioning is upstream of marketing-internal channels by construction; folding it into Axis 1 "Strategic Foundations" but then ALSO calling it a 6th upstream class is consistent with the live code's 6-class triage. **The concern:** the 6-class triage walks "retention/PMF → ICP/positioning → pricing → product activation → sales motion → marketing-internal" — but the v2 score-1 anchor lists "(retention/PMF, ICP, positioning, pricing model, product activation, sales motion)" with positioning as a SEPARATE item between ICP and pricing, not as part of a combined "ICP/positioning" class. The triage and the anchor enumeration disagree on count and grouping. Pick one. Currently the workflow gets to walk 5 OR 6 upstream classes depending on which sentence the judge attends to. Workflow will learn whichever cooperates with score-1.
- **Gap honesty folded in correctly.** "`gap_flagged` rubrics surface in `gap_report.md`; provider-blocked lenses are findings, not synthesis material" — this is the F6 invented-signals fold preserved correctly. **However:** `gap_report.md` exists in 3 of 4 current production sessions; the spec scopes MA-5 to depend on it. When the workflow doesn't emit it, MA-5's gap-honesty branch can't fire and the judge will hallucinate gap presence. Same substrate-readiness concern as §1.5.

**Voice-quality folded into §5 wrapper (not a criterion) — sufficient?** **TENTATIVELY YES, with a structural caveat.** The v2 wrapper carries "Voice quality applies across the artifact: prose has the voice quality of a customer-facing $15K+ agency artifact. Voice is consistent across sections (one author, not five). Em-dash density is restrained..." This is the F5 fold preserved from v1 — and it's the right call NOT to make voice a 6th criterion (would breach the 5-criterion ceiling without an LLM-failure-mode justification per design guide §5). **But:** wrapper-level voice guidance applies BEFORE per-criterion scoring; in the per-criterion CoT, voice never explicitly factors in. The judge will let voice influence its rationale tone but is not asked to score against voice. Workflow can ship technically-correct prose with high AI-tell density and structurally pass MA-1..MA-5; MA_BANNED_PHRASES structural_gate catches the worst tells but the broader voice-quality dimension is unmeasured. This is consistent with the design guide's "Hard Rules → structural_gate; Principles → judge" split, but the practical consequence is that voice-quality drift will not be detected by judge variance over generations. Acceptable if MA_BANNED_PHRASES is maintained aggressively; risky if the AI-tell vocabulary updates faster than the blocklist.

## §8 Completeness check — what's there + what's missing

§8 has 10 numbered items + hard constraints. Mostly thorough. Items 1 (multi-file structural_gate), 2 (calibration weighting), 3 (vertical fixture coverage), 5 (MA-1 ↔ MA-4 redundancy), 7 (first-cohort overfit watch), 10 (live-code restoration) are all genuinely load-bearing.

**What's missing:**

1. **Substrate-readiness gate.** §8 does not include "verify workflow emits all 5 required files in ≥1 fixture before locking the spec." The current state is 1-of-5 reliably + 1 sometimes + 3 never. Locking judge prose against a deliverable the substrate doesn't produce is the same coherence problem the design guide §2 ("the LLM judge sees the artifact only after structural_gate passes") explicitly warns against. **Recommend: add §8 item 11 — substrate emission verification — and gate ship on workflow producing roadmap.md + proposal.md + cuts_reduces_adds.md in ≥1 fixture end-to-end before MA v2 ships.**

2. **9-section / 12-axis dual-schema resolution.** §8 fold F8 declares both coexist; doesn't specify how the workflow synthesizes 12 from 9 without producing internally-repetitive output. Either fold the question into the open-questions list with an explicit "resolve before lock" trigger or drop one of the two schemas. **As written, the spec ships with a known organizational duplication.**

3. **Token-cost-per-generation × 50 generations math.** §8 q4 names $300–500/audit / $500 hard breaker — but does NOT tie to the v5 evolution-token postmortem (META $7.83/call, FIXTURE $1.82/call, WORKDIR $1.46/call). At 50 generations × 5 fixtures × 5 criteria × 3-model panel × 5–7-file deliverable, the cost shape is materially different from the current MA evolution cost. The cost-breaker for one *audit* doesn't tell us the cost of a full evolution run with the new spec. **This is the $2,521-in-75min repeat-pattern risk.** Recommend: cost-model a single-generation single-fixture-cohort run of MA v2 end-to-end and surface it as a §8 item.

4. **Polish first-cohort handling in the file-split layer.** §1 broadened the substitute-readers to US-primary; §1.5 multi-file deliverable does not have an equivalent broadening pass. Are Polish-language `findings.md` / `proposal.md` / `roadmap.md` files in scope? Klinika and DWF are first-cohort fixtures the spec validates against — and there's no statement about which language the multi-file deliverable ships in for Polish clients. This will become acute when the lane evaluates against a Polish fixture.

5. **Variance instrumentation per the design guide §11.5.** §8 doesn't reference the prescribed per-criterion variance tracking. MA has more criteria-folding-into-criteria than CI (MA-4 carries 4 dimensions; MA-5 carries 6+ failure modes); per-criterion variance is the only early-warning system the design guide accepts. Spec should commit to instrumenting variance per criterion per generation before evolution starts.

6. **MA-2 ↔ MA-3 redundancy is the more likely collapse, not MA-1 ↔ MA-4.** §8 q5 watches MA-1 ↔ MA-4 (both reward "diagnosis with evidence and locatability"). My concern is MA-2 (CUTS/REDUCES/ADDS map to capability tier with action+owner+budget+metric+kill-trigger) ↔ MA-3 (each recommendation traces to revenue mechanism with vertical-appropriate metric). Both score against recommendation completeness and revenue-traceability; both fail on vague verbs and missing metrics. The redundancy check should run BOTH pairs.

1. **Substrate-readiness mismatch ships → 100% of current sessions fail structural_gate on Day 1 → 50 generations spent climbing a substrate-emission cliff before any judge signal stabilizes.** The spec locks the judge against a multi-file deliverable that the workflow currently produces 1.5 of 5 files for on average. The evolution loop will spend its early generations trying to land structural_gate (which requires all 5 files present + within word-count bands) before any meaningful judge signal can emerge. **Mitigation: gate ship on substrate emission of ≥3 of 5 required files in ≥1 production fixture; OR scope the v2 ship to the 1-file substrate the workflow currently produces and defer the multi-file expansion to v3.** The current sequencing has the spec leading the workflow by a full implementation cycle and no §8 item names the gap.

2. **Commissioning-context routing (personnel / operational / strategic emphasis) is the rejected A/B/C cluster routing reborn at the criterion-prose layer → MA-4 element 4 carries it → workflow learns to slot-fill commissioning-context emphasis markers → Phase 4 feature-checking pathology recurs under a different name.** v2 explicitly rejected artifact-level routing; the same routing exists implicitly inside MA-4's score-1 anchor and §1's three commissioning shapes. The judge is asked to verify "detail emphasis matches commissioning context" without the fixture stating the commissioning context. 3. **9-section deliverable shell + 12-macro-axis structure coexist as dual-schema → workflow has to satisfy both simultaneously → output becomes internally repetitive (each finding labeled twice: under one of 9 sections AND one of 12 axes) → judge reads the repetition as comprehensiveness signal → workflow optimizes toward duplication → MA-6 voice-consistency wrapper guidance ("one author, not five") fails because the same content is rendered in two organizing frames within one file.** F8's "both coexist" is the architectural duplication. The structural_gate enforces 9 sections; the judge scores against 12 axes; the workflow's stable point is to render the diagnostic twice. **Mitigation: pick one organizing schema (the 12-axis is reader-facing and right; the 9-section is engineering-legacy from live code) and migrate STRUCTURAL_DOC_FACTS to enforce the 12 macro-axes as the new section spine.** This is a load-bearing live-code change, not a spec edit alone.

## Overall verdict

**NEEDS-EDIT, with two substrate-side gates that must close before ship.**

The criterion-prose layer (MA-1 through MA-5) is broadly in good shape — score-1 anchors are concrete, vertical examples discriminate, modern-lever bias on MA-2 is load-bearing and falsifiable, the 5-criterion ceiling is preserved without exception (research-predicted MA-6/MA-7 candidates correctly absorbed into MA-1 / MA-4 / MA-5), the 9 live-code surgical-restoration folds carry forward verbatim where they should. **Four judge-layer edits before ship:** (a) drop MA-4 element 4 (commissioning-context emphasis match) or move to structural_gate; (b) tighten MA-1 CoT Step 3 by moving severity-rollup-rule check to structural_gate; (c) resolve the MA-5 upstream-class enumeration disagreement (5 classes vs 6); (d) deduplicate the "12 canonical mediocre cuts" list (#2 and #5 are the same broetry-LinkedIn item).

**Two substrate-side gates that must close before ship:**

1. **Verify workflow emits ≥3 of the 5 required deliverable files in at least 1 production fixture end-to-end.** Current state is 1 reliably + 1 sometimes + 3 never. The judge spec is leading the substrate by a full implementation cycle. Either the workflow ships the multi-file emission first (preferred — gates judge spec on real artifacts) or the v2 spec scopes down to the 1-file substrate the workflow currently produces and defers multi-file expansion. The current §1.5 (d) claim "v006 has it wired" is materially incorrect for 3 of 5 files.

2. **Resolve the 9-section / 12-axis dual-schema before lock.** F8 declares both coexist; the implementation does not specify how. Workflow under selection pressure will produce internally-repetitive output (the same finding labeled twice under two organizing schemas) that the judge will read as comprehensiveness. Either migrate STRUCTURAL_DOC_FACTS to the 12-axis spine and retire the 9-section shell, OR retire the 12-axis framing and lock the spec against the 9-section live-code substrate. The "both coexist" handwave is the architectural duplication.

The §8 open-questions list is comprehensive on judge-internal concerns (redundancy, vertical coverage, calibration weighting, sibling-fork triggers) but thin on substrate-readiness, dual-schema resolution, cost-shape-at-50-generations, and Polish-language file emission. The most important missing question matches the CI v3.5 missing question: *what evidence do we have that the multi-file deliverable shape is what real MA clients actually consume?* The named exemplars (Kalungi, Bell Curve, ProfitWell, Wynter, Animalz) ship ONE deliverable per engagement, not five. If the honest answer is "none — research-derived," that's a reason to gate-flip files 2–5 to optional and ship findings.md only as production-default.

The modern-lever bias commitment (founder-led + LinkedIn/X + AEO + distribution-first + comparison-page warfare + named-stakeholder case studies + demo-direct CTAs) is the right anti-mediocre stance for 2026 tech-savvy-founder clients and is encoded correctly in MA-2's score-0 anchor + the 12 canonical mediocre cuts list. The Polish first-cohort overlay is correctly demoted from architectural target to concrete fixture. The 5-criterion ceiling is preserved.

**Bottom line:** MA v2 is taking the right correction (away from rejected cluster routing, toward comprehensive 12-axis audit with modern-lever bias) but at v2's current locking depth, the spec is downstream-of-substrate in a way the design guide explicitly warns against, AND it carries the rejected routing in disguise at the MA-4 + §1-commissioning-context layer. The path forward is: (1) ship workflow-side multi-file emission first; (2) resolve dual-schema; (3) drop commissioning-context routing from MA-4; (4) THEN lock the spec. Approving the spec to ship at this draft would commit ~50 evolution generations against a substrate that doesn't produce the deliverable the spec scores.
