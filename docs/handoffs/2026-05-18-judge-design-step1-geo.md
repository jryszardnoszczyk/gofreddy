# GEO (Generative Engine Optimization / AEO) — Optimal-Output Spec (DRAFT v2)

Conforms to `docs/rubrics/judge-design-guide.md` with one documented exception (§7). Frameworks
(Aggarwal KDD 2024, Volpini Matryoshka Paragraph, Shepard 2025, Kalicube Entity SEO, Yext 2026,
Profound Index, BLUF, AEO) inform the reader/success/failure spec and are the judge's reasoning
toolkit. They do NOT appear by name in criterion prose.

This v2 supersedes v1 by restructuring the artifact-scope envelope. v1 treated GEO as
single-landing-page optimization across 10 form factors. The May-19 comprehensive-scope research
(`docs/research/2026-05-19-geo-comprehensive-scope.md`) maps **22 distinct GEO-relevant activity
axes across 6 layers** that a 2026 AI-native agency actually owns: Page-level (5 axes, v1 covers);
Site architecture (4 axes); Structured-data + AEO infrastructure (3 axes); Off-page signal
engineering (5 axes — the single largest v1 gap); Distribution + AI-engine-native channels (4
axes); Monitoring + defense + measurement (5 axes). v1 covered ~5 of 22 — losing ~70% of the
2026 ceiling.

v2 expands the lane's deliverable surface to a **comprehensive GEO Strategic Plan** — a single
coherent 12,000-18,000-word multi-component bundle across 12 named sections (§A audit → §L
appendices) — while keeping the rubric architecture (6 outcome criteria, AND-conjunction at each,
structural_gate routes verifiables) **unchanged**. The judge continues to score the **Page-level
core component** (Section §C plus the on-page parts of §A audit and §I bio engineering) via the
6 outcome questions; structural_gate validates the outer envelope (§B / §D / §E / §F / §G / §H /
§J / §K / §L) via per-section presence + word-count band + format-of-deliverable checks. This is
the **School B architecture**: multi-component bundle, judge focuses on the core component,
structural_gate validates non-core components, criteria-count discipline preserved.

The modern-lever bias is now explicit throughout. §3 catalogues 20 deprecated SEO tactics that
contribute zero or negative value to 2026 AI citation (keyword density, mass directory
submissions, AMP, EMD plays, link-building outreach, schema stuffing, page-speed obsession beyond
table stakes, content velocity for its own sake, generic listicles, PBN tactics, reciprocal
linking, etc.) — these are CUTS the strategic plan must call out. §3 also catalogues 15 modern
AEO levers that did not exist or were not load-bearing in 2018 (comparison-page warfare at 43.8%
ChatGPT citation share; brand-mention engineering at r=0.664 vs r=0.218 backlinks; Reddit answer-
shape engineering at 20.8% top-50 citation share; Wikipedia + Wikidata anchoring; llms.txt +
llms-full.txt + per-page markdown siblings; Custom GPT / Claude Project / Gemini Gem publication;
agentic-commerce protocols ACP/UCP/MCP; Knowledge Panel + GBP seeding; per-engine retrieval
testing; founder-bio E-E-A-T construction; original first-party research; speakable schema;
per-vertical community-engagement maps; negative-AEO defense). These are ADDS the strategic plan
must operationalize.

"Looks elaborate" ≠ "over-engineered." The comprehensive-deliverable shift is justified by
documented economic value (each top-3 modern lever individually outweighs most cuts per
`docs/research/2026-05-19-geo-comprehensive-scope.md` §3). The judge-layer architecture remains
minimal: 6 outcome criteria, AND-conjunction at each, structural_gate routes verifiables.
Expansion of *what the workflow produces* does not require expansion of *what the judge counts* —
that's the School B move.

## 1. Reader (LOCKED 2026-05-18, BROADENED 2026-05-19)

GEO content serves TWO readers simultaneously, both primary. The judge must score for both via
AND-conjunction at each criterion (§5 wrapper makes this explicit).

**Primary human reader.** A founder, head of marketing, product lead, head of strategy, or
operations lead at a US-primary tech-savvy company that has commissioned a comprehensive GEO
strategic plan ahead of an AI-search-citation push. The reader will act on the prescription —
ship the page-level optimizations, route the off-page signal plan to PR / community managers,
implement the structured-data and llms.txt infrastructure, claim Knowledge Panels, build out
Custom GPT / Claude Project distribution, stand up cross-engine retrieval monitoring. They are
smart, time-poor, and skeptical — they have been pitched enough SEO-pivoted-to-AEO content to
recognize slot-fills. They will not re-read; they get one extraction pass and then either commit
budget / engineering capacity / PR cycles to the plan, or they shelve it.

Specifically, the load-bearing human-reader personas:

- **Founder or head of marketing at a small-medium B2B SaaS** (10-500 employees, post-series-A
  through pre-IPO) pursuing AI-search-citation share against named category competitors. They
  convert at the AI-traffic baseline (~14% — five times Google organic per Adobe / RankScience
  Q1-Q2 2026 data) when their content reads as reference, not as marketing.
- **Founder or head of research at an AI lab or developer-tools company** (Anthropic / Cursor /
  Perplexity / similar) pursuing developer + analyst + AI-engine retrieval share. They lean on
  Mintlify-style docs, llms-full.txt, arxiv anchoring, named-researcher visibility, and
  comparison content against named alternatives.
- **Partner or principal at an agency or consulting firm** (gofreddy itself; mid-market
  consulting / professional-services firms) pursuing head-to-head comparison editorial,
  named-author insight pieces, podcast appearances, and per-vertical specialization signaling.
- **Owner-operator or operations lead at a service business** (medical / dental / aesthetic /
  legal / accounting / financial-planning / hospitality / specialty trades) pursuing local-pack
  capture, Google Business Profile anchoring, niche-community engagement, and clinician /
  attorney / advisor E-E-A-T construction. Skepticism calibrated against YMYL gating norms for
  healthcare / legal / fintech.
- **Head of marketing or compliance officer at a finance / fintech company** (US-primary SEC /
  FINRA / CFPB regulated; UK FCA; Polish KNF) pursuing regulator-aligned definitional content,
  per-jurisdiction scope disclosure, compliance-officer bylines, and YMYL-appropriate authority
  signaling.
- **Founder or head of growth at a DTC e-commerce / consumer brand** pursuing product / offer /
  aggregate-rating schema, ACP / UCP integration for ChatGPT-resident commerce, UGC review
  surfacing (Reddit, YouTube, niche-community), and per-product structured data. The AI search
  engine is the literal point-of-sale interface in 2026 for an increasing fraction of consumer
  queries.

The human reader has authority to act on what they read: route a budget, brief a PR firm,
commission a Custom GPT, file a Wikidata correction, allocate engineering capacity to schema
implementation. They have the option to NOT act — and a comprehensive plan that doesn't earn the
"yes" gets shelved.

**Primary machine reader.** The AI search engine itself — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini,
Google AI Mode, Copilot, Bing — deciding whether to retrieve a passage from the client's pages
into the candidate set AND whether to cite it in the final synthesized answer. The engine operates
a four-stage pipeline (Volpini's framing): lexical-candidate-generation → dense retrieval →
reranking → synthesis-with-citation. It fans out the user's query into multiple sub-queries,
retrieves candidates across the fan-out set, reranks by passage relevance and authority, and
synthesizes with citation footnotes.

Engine behavior is empirically measured: ChatGPT cites Wikipedia (#1 source globally, 7.8% of all
citations) and Reddit (20.8% top-50 share) most; listicles account for 43.8% of all ChatGPT
citations (Ahrefs 26,283-URL study); Perplexity cites sources with visible publication or update
dates within 12-18 months 70% of the time, 78% of complex-research answers tie every claim to a
specific source; 90% of top-cited Perplexity sources answer the core question within the first
100 words; Google AI Overviews now pull only 38% of citations from Google's organic top-10 (down
from 76% in July 2025) and only 12% of all AI-cited URLs overlap with Google's top-10 on the
original prompt — AI citation is diverging from organic ranking. Brand mentions correlate r=0.664
with AI Overview visibility; backlinks correlate only r=0.218 (Ahrefs 75K-brand study) — a 3×
priority-stack flip vs the 2018 link-building playbook.

The engine reader is the gating reader: pages an AI engine won't cite reach no human. The page
must be citation-worthy on its own merits, not on link-graph inheritance — and it must be
citation-RESILIENT to the engine's own hallucination tendencies (entity confabulation, source
confab, recency distortion, inverted-citation attack, competitive injection — see §3 and GEO-6).

**Substitute readers the same comprehensive plan should also serve.** Founder-CEO doing
competitive-landscape research before a quarterly off-site; corp-dev / strategy lead evaluating
a category for acquisition; head of product at an early-co launching into a new vertical; head
of marketing at a regulated-finance firm evaluating compliance-aligned content; head of growth
at a hospitality / retail / DTC brand pursuing agentic-commerce share; founder at a niche-vertical
SaaS evaluating per-engine retrieval positioning; head of communications at a YMYL practice
(healthcare / legal / fintech) building author / founder bio E-E-A-T architecture.

**Polish-language first-cohort context, not architectural target.** Klinika Melitus (Warsaw
aesthetic dermatology) and DWF Poland are concrete first-cohort fixtures that test the spec
against (a) Polish-language content for Perplexity / ChatGPT routing-by-locale to Polish corpora,
(b) European / Polish-regulator citation conventions (KNF, Komisja Etyki Lekarskiej, Naczelna
Rada Adwokacka), and (c) local-pack capture against Polish-language community substrates (Wykop
for general; per-vertical communities in PL). These are **concrete anchors**, not architectural
defaults. The spec is designed to generalize to US-primary tech-savvy-founder / early-co clients
across SaaS / AI-lab / agency / service-business / finance / e-commerce verticals; first-cohort
overfit is an explicit risk to monitor (see §8). Aggarwal KDD 2024 is load-bearing here: the
optimal optimization method is domain-conditioned — Statistics Addition wins for Law &
Government / Opinion; Quotation Addition wins for People & Society / History; Fluency
Optimization wins for Health and Business. Single-formula GEO is wrong by construction.

**NOT the reader.** A brand-loyalist already on the site looking for product detail; a journalist
sourcing a specific named expert; a developer reading API reference for an integration task; a
casual browser without query intent. These readers exist; this artifact is not for them. NOT the
reader for the strategic plan: the operator who will actually ship the page-level changes (that's
a downstream-of-plan implementation reader); the analytics person who will measure citation share
weekly (that's the monitoring lane); the PR firm that will execute podcast appearances (that's a
contracted operator implementing the plan). The plan reader is the **decision-maker on the
budget envelope and prioritization**.

**First-cohort posture (v3 explicit).** Klinika Melitus and DWF Poland are the only two onboarded
clients (both Polish-language, both regulated-vertical — aesthetic dermatology and law). The
US-primary substitute readers enumerated above are the **architectural target** as the client
base expands Q3-2026+; they are NOT first-cohort fixtures. Per the design-guide cohort-expansion
discipline: Polish-language fixture passes required before v3 lock against any Klinika or DWF
session; US-primary fixture passes (B2B SaaS, AI-lab, DTC e-commerce, regulated finance, agency)
required for architectural-target validation. The straddle — concrete Polish-language fixtures
exercising the rubric today, US-primary substitute-readers shaping the rubric for tomorrow — is
**intentional during cohort expansion** and is not a contradiction. See §8 question 9 for
re-validation triggers when new verticals land.

## 1.5. Artifact shape — comprehensive GEO Strategic Plan (LOCKED 2026-05-19, School B architecture)

**v1 framing (single landing page in one of 10 form factors) is the right MVP but the wrong
ceiling.** Per `docs/research/2026-05-19-geo-comprehensive-scope.md` §1, a 2026 AI-native agency
serving the substitute-reader mix above is responsible for at least **22 distinct GEO-relevant
activity axes across 6 layers** — only ~5 of which are single-page concerns. The remaining ~17
(site architecture, off-page signal engineering, comparison-page warfare, brand-mention
engineering, retrieval testing, cross-engine optimization, custom-GPT distribution, knowledge-
panel capture, author / founder E-E-A-T construction, negative-AEO defense, agentic-commerce
integration, multilingual / international AEO) are where the differentiated 2026 wins live.

**v2 deliverable: ONE comprehensive multi-component "GEO Strategic Plan" bundle per fixture**,
not 12 separate artifacts and not a single page. Single coherent ~12,000-18,000-word artifact
that the evolution loop iterates on as a whole.

**The 12 named sections (§A through §L):**

- **§A — Current-state AEO audit** (1,500-2,500 words). Per-axis scoring across all 22 axes
  for the client's current substrate. Identifies what's present, what's missing, what's
  deprecated and should be removed.
- **§B — Cut / Reduce / Add prescription** (800-1,200 words). Explicit list of the deprecated
  tactics the client is currently running that contribute zero or negative value and should be
  CUT. Explicit list of the modern AEO levers the client is not yet running and should ADD.
  Reasoning + sequencing for each.
- **§C — Page-level optimization recommendations + sample sections** (1,500-2,500 words). The
  core component the judge scopes. Per-page recommendations using the per-fixture `geo_format`
  enum routing (10 form factors below). Includes 1-3 sample optimized passages or page
  fragments showing the modern declarative-document register applied to the client's actual
  content.
- **§D — Site-architecture recommendations** (800-1,500 words). Entity graph + knowledge-graph
  anchoring (schema.org sameAs to Wikidata / Crunchbase / SEC EDGAR / LinkedIn / GBP);
  pillar-and-cluster hub topology (with 2026 caveats — thin pillars demoted by Google March /
  April 2026 updates); internal-linking strategy + breadcrumbs; canonical URL / sitemap / robots
  hygiene including AI-crawler permissions (OAI-SearchBot / PerplexityBot / ChatGPT-User /
  GPTBot / Google-Extended / Claude-Web / CCBot). Recommendations the GEO plan specifies;
  `site_engine` lane implements.
- **§E — Off-page signal plan** (1,500-2,500 words). The single biggest gap in v1. Reddit
  answer-shape recommendations (subreddit list, comment-quality criteria, escalation rules,
  founder-vs-team assignment, monthly cadence) — NOT astroturfing, real-account engagement.
  Wikipedia editing targets + Wikidata Q-ID seeding (Polish Wikipedia / Wikidata also for PL
  clients). Quora + StackExchange + niche-community map (Indie Hackers, Hacker News, dev.to,
  Lobsters, RealSelf, Avvo, WebMD forums — per-vertical). PR + podcast appearance + founder
  visibility targets (Tier-1 broad podcasts; Tier-2 vertical podcasts; Tier-1 PR placements per
  vertical; LinkedIn / Twitter posting cadence). Citation building (.gov / .edu / journal /
  industry-association mentions) where the vertical supports it.
- **§F — Comparison-page warfare plan** (800-1,500 words). Target list (who to compare against),
  framing playbook per comparison, publishing cadence, monitoring of competitor "vs us" pages.
  Per Ahrefs 26K-URL study: 43.8% of all ChatGPT citations go to listicles / comparison /
  alternatives pages. Xero, NetSuite, Sage Intacct, HubSpot, Linear playbook documented.
- **§G — Distribution + AI-engine-native plan** (800-1,200 words). Custom GPT / Claude Project /
  Gemini Gem / Perplexity Space publication (system prompt + knowledge sources + analytics
  monitoring). Agentic-commerce protocol integration (ACP for DTC ChatGPT resident commerce; UCP
  for Google AI Mode Walmart / Target / Shopify / Etsy integration; MCP tool definitions for
  B2B SaaS with developer surface). Knowledge Panel + Google Business Profile + Bing Places
  claim and optimization. Multilingual / international AEO (hreflang + per-language schema +
  per-language Wikidata + per-locale community substrates for international + Polish-language
  clients).
- **§H — Cross-engine retrieval testing plan** (600-1,000 words). Per-client retrieval dashboard
  design (Profound / Ahrefs Brand Radar / Otterly / AthenaHQ / Peec AI / SE Ranking AI
  Visibility / Semrush AI Toolkit — which to use, why, cost envelope). Per-engine optimization
  tactics (ChatGPT favors Wikipedia + Reddit; Perplexity favors freshness 12-18mo + 21+ source
  citation density; Claude favors official docs + low-citation-fab → over-cites primary sources;
  Google AI Mode now pulls 38% from outside organic top-10; Gemini deferential to Google
  Knowledge Graph). Weekly digest design. monitoring lane runs this operationally.
- **§I — Author / founder bio engineering recommendations** (400-800 words). Per-author bio
  pages with credentials, dated history, named publications, linked sameAs to Wikidata /
  LinkedIn / Crunchbase / ORCID / academic-institution profile; Person schema; consistent name
  across all bio surfaces. Per-vertical: clinician bios with board credentials for healthcare;
  attorney bios with bar number + jurisdiction + practice areas for legal; researcher bios with
  arxiv author IDs + GitHub + publication history for AI-labs; compliance-officer bios with
  regulator-license disclosure for fintech. **This is the load-bearing E-E-A-T signal in 2026
  — the deliverable is the bio architecture, not just the bio prose.**
- **§J — Negative-AEO defense + brand-reputation monitoring** (400-800 words). Third-party
  content monitoring for brand mentions; inverted-citation attack flagging; remediation
  playbook (corrections via outlet editors; Wikipedia correction; counter-evidence publication;
  legal escalation for libel). For YMYL clients (healthcare / legal / fintech): mis-attributed
  regulatory claims are compliance issues, not just SEO issues.
- **§K — 30/60/90 execution roadmap** (600-1,000 words). Named owners + measurable outcomes per
  phase. Day 0-30: foundation + diagnostics + structural fixes + Wikidata seed + retrieval
  baseline. Day 31-60: page + site execution + comparison-page warfare ships + bios shipped
  + Custom GPT published. Day 61-90: off-page signal execution + Knowledge Panel claims +
  retrieval dashboard live + negative-AEO monitoring live.
- **§L — Appendices** (1,500-2,500 words). Schema markup snippets ready to deploy per form
  factor. llms.txt + llms-full.txt template. Sample Reddit answer-shape. Sample Wikipedia edit.
  Sample comparison-page outline. Banned-phrase blocklist. Per-vertical evidence-substrate
  reference list (statutes / clinical guidelines / regulators / analyst sources).

**Per-section budget total: 12,000-18,000 words** with 20,000-word ceiling enforced by
`structural_gate`. Larger than v1's single-page artifact; smaller than 12 separate artifacts.
Coherent end-to-end; granular enough that each section has clear local quality.

**Page-level form-factor enum (preserved from v1, now scoped to Section §C).** The 10 canonical
page-type form factors continue to route per-fixture via `geo_format` declared on the fixture
brief. The judge stays format-agnostic at the criterion layer; `structural_gate` enforces
form-factor-specific shape conformance within Section §C.

1. **Definition** (`definition`) — "What is X" entity-grounding; 600-1,500 words; declarative
   lead in first 40-75 words; Article + DefinedTerm schema.
2. **How-to** (`how_to`) — Procedural; 800-2,500 words; numbered steps + prerequisites +
   verification + troubleshooting; HowTo schema only when primary purpose (Google March 2026
   demotion of non-primary HowTo).
3. **Comparison** (`comparison`) — "X vs Y" head-to-head; 1,200-2,500 words; ≥1 comparison table
   with ≥3 dimensions; dual-entity symmetry; both entities cited at parity.
4. **Listicle / alternatives** (`listicle`) — "Best of X" / "Alternatives to Y"; 2,000-4,500
   words; ranked items with disclosed methodology; per-item 100-200 words + structured row;
   ItemList + (optionally) FAQPage schema. **Dominant 2026 citation form factor — 43.8% of
   ChatGPT citations.**
5. **FAQ** (`faq`) — Standalone or embedded; question-as-heading + 40-75 word answer; FAQPage
   schema (Google killed FAQ rich-results May 7 2026 — schema is now AI-engine-only signal,
   not SERP-feature signal; 3.2× more likely to appear in AI Overviews per Frase 2026).
6. **Glossary entry** (`glossary`) — Atomic 200-500 word definition; DefinedTerm in
   DefinedTermSet; cross-linked into glossary index.
7. **Pillar / topic hub** (`pillar_hub`) — Broad-category orchestration; 1,500-3,500 words; 4-8
   sub-topic sections; original-research / data block. **CAUTION:** Google March / April 2026
   updates demoted hub-bloat / intermediary-content / topical-bloat — boundary with `site_engine`
   lane needs adjudication.
8. **Integration page** (`integration`) — "Tool X + Tool Y"; 600-1,400 words; workflow + setup
   + limits + supported APIs; Article + SoftwareApplication schema.
9. **Pricing page** (`pricing`) — Public tier matrix; 800-1,800 words including FAQ tail;
   server-rendered HTML with SoftwareApplication + Offer schema; concrete prices in schema.
10. **Product / category landing page** (`product_landing`) — Brand-anchored entity definition
    + category placement + third-party validation block.

**The School B architecture: who judges what.** Per the design-guide §2 split (Hard Rules →
`structural_gate`, Principles → judge), v2 routes the comprehensive deliverable as follows:

- **The LLM judge scopes the Page-level core component** — Section §C (page-level optimization
  recommendations + sample sections) plus the on-page parts of §A (audit findings on the page-
  level axes 1-5) plus §I (author / founder bio architecture as load-bearing E-E-A-T signal).

## 3. Failure — mediocre, deprecated-tactic Goodhart, and AI-specific failure surfaces (LOCKED 2026-05-19)

### 3a. Mediocre — page-level failure modes the judge must discriminate against

**Query-mirroring lead.** Page opens with "What is X? X is becoming increasingly important…"
(Volpini's Matryoshka Paragraph: under Google's asymmetric embedding scheme this signals
"another query" not "an answer," and ranks behind sources that lead with a declarative
definition; Aggarwal documents keyword-stuffing as the single most consistent negative signal).

**Buried answer.** Core claim sits below the fold or in paragraph 4. Generative engines have
retrieval caps per URL — 44% of all AI citations come from the top third of a page; if the
answer isn't there, the citation isn't either.

**Prose where a table would do.** Comparison content, pricing, feature differences, spec lists
written as flowing paragraphs lose to the same data structured as a table by 4.2× (Profound).

**Unsourced specificity.** A number without a source is a marketing claim, not a fact.
Aggarwal's Statistics Addition method works ONLY when statistics are presented as verifiable.

**Floating pronouns and orphan passages.** Volpini's chunk-complete principle — if a 50-word
passage extracted standalone can't be understood without prior context ("This makes it…",
"The above shows…"), the retrieval system treats it as low-confidence material.

**Vendor self-puffery without third-party signal.** Forrester 2026: AI buyers validate vendor
claims against external sources before trusting them. Pages that don't acknowledge the
competitive landscape read as marketing not reference. The Verge documented Google AI Mode
catching and de-ranking vendor-authored "best of" lists that placed their own products first.

**Stale freshness.** No visible date, no current-year reference, no recent third-party
citation. Perplexity 70% / 12-18 month finding; Ahrefs 17M-citation freshness study.

**Entity drift.** "Acme Pay" / "Acme Payments" / "AcmePay" across sections. Embeddings cluster
these as different entities; citation signal fragments.

**Mismatch between page intent and likely query class.** A "/best-X-for-Y" URL written as
flowing marketing narrative; a "/how-to-X" page without ordered steps; a "/X-vs-Y" page that's
actually a single-sided product page. Shepard's Intent-Format Match (#6, 9.0).

### 3b. Deprecated-tactic CUTS — what the plan must explicitly remove (modern-lever bias)

The 2018-2022 SEO playbook contains ~20 tactics that contribute zero or negative value to 2026
AI citation. A strategic plan that does NOT explicitly call out these cuts is shipping a status-
quo continuation, not a 2026 prescription. The plan must surface and CUT, with reasoning, at
least the high-priority items relevant to the client's current substrate:

- **CUT 1 — Keyword density / exact-match repetition.** Aggarwal KDD 2024 documents keyword
  stuffing as a *negative* signal. The 2018 "use the keyword 5-7 times per body content" rule
  actively hurts in 2026.
- **CUT 2 — Exact-match anchor text in internal links.** Engines extract semantic relationships,
  not anchor-text matches. Descriptive anchor outperforms keyword-only anchor.
- **CUT 3 — Mass directory submissions.** DMOZ-era tactic; most directory citations de-weighted.
  Vertical-specific exceptions only (Avvo / RealSelf / G2 / Crunchbase).
- **CUT 4 — Generic listicles (templated AI-generated roundups).** Google's April 2026 update
  demoted templated AI-roundup listicles by 25-35% in competitive niches.

  (Ahrefs 75K-brand study). Cold-outreach-for-do-follow-link is 3× less efficient than the
  equivalent PR or podcast effort.
- **CUT 6 — Schema stuffing without substance.** FAQPage on pages without organic FAQ; Review
  on pages without visible reviews; HowTo on non-primary content (Google March 2026 demotion).
  Teaches AI engines to distrust the domain.
- **CUT 7 — AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages).** Functionally end-of-life. Migrate to standard
  mobile.
- **CUT 8 — EMD (exact-match domain) plays.** "bestcrm.com" / "warsaw-derm.pl" tactics dead
  since 2012; AI engines weight even less.
- **CUT 9 — Content velocity for its own sake.** "4 blog posts per week" goalposting. AI
  engines preferentially cite a smaller number of authoritative pages. Helpful Content
  classifier penalizes thin-content density.
- **CUT 10 — Page-speed obsession beyond table stakes.** LCP < 2.5s is table stakes; beyond
  that, Core Web Vitals optimization is diminishing returns for AI citation.
- **CUT 11 — Generic FAQ pages.** Google killed FAQ rich-results May 7 2026. Standalone FAQ
  pages mostly dead; FAQ as appendix still useful.
- **CUT 12 — Pure-product-storytelling pages without functional attributes.** DTC "our story +

  capacity, use case, named specs) earn citation.
- **CUT 13 — Pillar pages with thin sub-section stubs (hub bloat).** Google March / April 2026
  demoted.
- **CUT 14 — Auto-generated meta descriptions.** AI engines extract from body content, not meta.
- **CUT 15 — Glossary bloat (hundreds of thin entries).** Demoted by Google March 2026 as
  intermediary-content / topical bloat.
- **CUT 16 — Reciprocal linking schemes.** End-of-life same era as directory submissions.
- **CUT 17 — PBN (Private Blog Network) tactics.** Black-hat; AI engines actively de-rank.
- **CUT 18 — Schema-only signals without on-page support.** 5-star Review schema on a page
  with no visible reviews is one of the fastest ways to erode AI trust.
- **CUT 19 — Targeting "featured snippet" position 0.** Featured snippets displaced by AI
  Overviews; target shifts from SERP feature to retrieval-shape.
- **CUT 20 — Mass guest-post campaigns.** Per CUT 5 — don't kill all guest posts, but the
  "publish on 50 low-tier blogs for a do-follow link" mechanic is dead.

### 3d. Goodhart-collapse — Phase-4 pathology + per-vertical modes + AI-specific failure surfaces

**Phase-4 pathology (the historical Goodhart trap).** 50-generation evolution against a feature-
checking judge produces exactly the pathology rolled back at HEAD `c76f051` (commit `698e658`).
The workflow learns to slot-fill the verifiable surface markers:

- **Citation stuffing.** Three citations per section regardless of relevance.
- **Stats-table cargo-cult.** A stats table dropped on every page regardless of whether the
  page shape calls for one.
- **"Last updated" date gaming.** Stamp touched daily by automation with zero body-content
  changes.
- **Entity-name spam.** Brand name repeated 12+ times per section without canonical
  disambiguation in the lead.
- **Exactly-40-word answer-bait passages.** Plants 40-word "answer-shaped" passages regardless
  of whether the substance supports them.
- **Schema markup slot-fill.** JSON-LD describing content the page doesn't actually carry.
- **Methodology-block-without-substance.** Listicle discloses criteria with no evidence the
  criteria were applied to ranked items.
- **§B cut/add slot-fill (NEW under v2 multi-component scope).** Workflow learns to populate
  §B with the full 20-cut + 15-add list verbatim regardless of which apply to the actual
  client; the section reads as a template recitation rather than a client-specific
  prescription. Defense: `structural_gate` validates §B presence + word-count band; the
  judge at GEO-5 tests format-intent match including whether technical recommendations are
  concrete client-specific counts vs vague boilerplate.

AI engines catch most of these within 1-2 indexing cycles — the page enters the "looks AEO-
optimized therefore distrust" tier. The page scores high on a feature-checking judge AND low
on actual AI citation. The judge has to test for OUTCOMES (would the engine cite, would the
human act on the comprehensive prescription) not for surface-marker presence.

### GEO-1 — Answer-first BLUF compliance (AND-conjunction: extractable form AND substantive claim)

**Outcome question (binary):**
Does the §C page-level recommendation surface a primary claim — what the product / service /
entity is, who it serves, what makes it different — in the first 40-75 words of meaningful body
content, in declarative-document register (not query-echo register), AND does that 40-75-word
passage carry a substantive claim a domain expert in the page's target vertical would defend?
Would an AI engine extracting the top passage emit a complete, citable answer that a
sophisticated human reader would also accept as reference-grade?

**Score 1 (yes)** — First 40-75 words of the §C sample page (or the recommended BLUF lead for
the audited client page) contain BOTH (a) a declarative entity definition + category placement
+ a differentiation claim in retrieval-document register (no interrogative opener, no brand
storytelling preamble, no deprecated keyword-density-targeted lede), AND (b) a substantive
claim that names the specific vertical / target reader / non-generic differentiator that a
domain expert would defend. An AI engine could emit those 75 words verbatim AND a sophisticated
human reader would not classify the page as "generic AI content" on the strength of the first
passage alone. The passage works as standalone AND fits the page's existing voice, structure,
and scope — content reads like it was always there, not bolted on. Surgical-content-injection
test: a developer reading the brief could ship the passage without interpretation, and a
returning reader who knew the prior page voice would not perceive a register break.

independently, AND would a sophisticated human researcher trust those sources as off-domain and
reference-grade? Does the evidence type match the page's vertical (the Aggarwal domain-

discipline, not the example value). Off-domain attribution also includes competitor wins where

### GEO-5 — Format-intent match and vertical-conditioned freshness (AND-conjunction: format match AND substantive freshness at vertical cadence)

**Outcome question (binary):**
Does the §C page-level recommendation's structure match the format AI engines prefer for its
declared query class (declared via `geo_format` on the fixture brief, cross-checkable via URL
slug / page title / H1) — comparison → table; how-to → ordered steps; what-is → definition +
structured detail; listicle → ranked items with methodology — AND does it carry the freshness
signals AI engines weight at the vertical-appropriate cadence (substantive currency in body
content, not just date-stamp gaming)?

**Score 1 (yes)** — BOTH (a) page format matches its `geo_format` declaration and likely query
class (visible from URL slug, page title, or H1 — a `/best-X-for-Y` listicle page is structured
as a ranked list with at least one comparison table and disclosed methodology; a `/how-to-X`
page is structured as ordered steps with prerequisites + verification + troubleshooting
blocks; a `/what-is-X` definition page leads with a declarative entity definition); the page
directly answers the target queries declared on the brief — informational queries get
explanations, commercial queries get comparisons, transactional queries get pricing and next
steps. Intent-mismatch test (do not optimize toward the specific brand; the test is the
intent-class discipline): a page optimized for "how much does Ahrefs cost" must surface pricing
in the first substantive passage, not company history; a transactional-intent page that
answers with informational-intent prose fails regardless of structural quality. AND (b)
freshness signal is substantive at the vertical-appropriate cadence:

- **DTC pricing / shopping queries** — current-week date stamp; pricing visible in initial
  server-rendered HTML (not JS-only per Similarweb 2026 case study); inventory / availability
  dated.
- **Fintech rates / yields / regulatory** — current-month date stamp; rate / fee / APY data
  dated within 30 days; regulator-reference cited with current version.
- **B2B SaaS feature / pricing / comparison** — current-quarter date stamp; feature claims
  dated within 90 days; pricing visible server-rendered HTML.
- **Healthcare evidence-based explainer** — last-medically-reviewed within 24 months on stable
  conditions, within 90 days on emerging treatments; named-guideline citation with current
  version.
- **Legal statute / case citation** — last-reviewed within 12 months; statute-version-bound
  (a 1998 statute citation with a 2026 last-reviewed stamp is correct, not stale); case
  citation with current Shepard / KeyCite-equivalent reliability check.
- **AI-lab API / SDK documentation** — per-release date stamp; version-pinned code examples;
  dated changelog; benchmark cards with reproduction methodology + date.
- **Evergreen explainer content (general)** — visible publication or update date within last
  12-18 months (the Perplexity 70% / 12-18 month finding holds as default).

**Score 0 (no)** — Format mismatch (a comparison page written as flowing narrative; a how-to
page without ordered steps; a listicle without disclosed methodology). No visible date
anywhere. Stats without years. OR "Last updated YYYY-MM-DD" current-year stamp on body content
with no current-year references, named-current-version-citation, or substantive freshness
signal (the workflow has gamed the stamp — §3d Goodhart mode). OR freshness window is wrong
for the vertical (a DTC pricing page with a 12-month stamp; a fintech rate page with a
quarterly stamp). OR when the page recommendation carries technical guidance (audit-derived
findings, fix lists in §A), it is vague boilerplate rather than concrete counts and named
specifics — "21 of 22 images lack alt text" is actionable; "consider adding alt text to
images" is decoration. Boilerplate technical recommendations that don't reference real problems
found on the actual page fail. OR the recommendation perpetuates a deprecated format-intent
mismatch (CUT 11 — generic FAQ page; CUT 19 — featured-snippet position-0 targeting; CUT 7 —
AMP page).

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Format matches but freshness signal is ambiguous (e.g., date present
but more than the vertical-appropriate window stale on a page making current-state claims; OR
cadence ambiguous from the artifact alone). Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which
dimension is weak.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Identify the page's declared `geo_format` (from fixture brief, cross-checked against
  URL / title / H1 query class).
- Step 2: Verify format matches that declared class (comparison → table; how-to → ordered
  steps; listicle → ranked items + methodology; definition → declarative lead + structured
  detail).
- Step 3: Identify freshness signals (publication date, last-updated, current-year refs, dated
  third-party citations) and test against the vertical-appropriate cadence.
- Step 4: Test whether freshness is substantive (body content matches stamp) or stamped-only
  (gaming).
- Step 5: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

### GEO-6 — Evidence chain survives engine-side re-citation (NEW in v1 — documented ≤5-ceiling exception)

**Outcome question (binary):**
If an AI engine were to retrieve a passage from the §C sample page (or from the audited
client's actual page per §A) and synthesize an answer with one or two of the documented LLM
failure modes — similar-name conflation, source-anchor hallucination, partial-attribute
corruption, recency-cutoff distortion, or competitor-favorable reframing — would the brand
still come out correctly identified, correctly attributed, correctly time-framed, and not
out-framed by a competitor? Does the page's structure FORCE the engine toward correct
synthesis rather than relying on the engine to figure it out? Are the top-3 strategic claims
on the page each backed by named signals, verifiable sources, and acknowledged alternative
interpretations?

**Score 1 (yes)** — Page recommendation contains ALL of:

(a) **Disambiguation against similar-name confusables** — explicit disambiguation block early
when the entity has a most-confusable similar-name target ("Anthropic, the AI safety lab
founded 2021 — not Anthropic Communications LLC"; "Cursor, the AI-native IDE — not Cursor Inc.
the eye-tracking device"). Singleton canonical name across H1 + schema.org `@id` + OpenGraph +
BLUF.

(b) **KG anchor for inverted-citation-attack prophylaxis** — schema.org `sameAs` to at least
one canonical KG entry (Wikidata > Crunchbase > LinkedIn Company > Google Business Profile >
registry .gov, tiered for SMB).

(c) **Top-3 claims with named signals + verifiable sources + acknowledged alternatives** — the
headline, the dominant-positioning claim, and the strongest differentiation claim each (i)
name the specific signals they rest on, (ii) cite verifiable off-domain sources, AND (iii)
acknowledge at least one alternative interpretation the evidence does NOT rule out. Confidence
is calibrated to evidence depth.

(d) **Absolute-date framing for all temporal claims** so engines reasoning about "recent"
don't conflate with training-cutoff "recent" — no "recently," "in recent months," "today's,"
"the latest" without absolute-date qualifier.

(e) **Comparison-claim symmetry where competitors are named** — every competitor claim
(numeric, dated, quoted) backed by off-domain citation; no asymmetry where brand claims are
supported and competitor claims are unsupported (or vice versa); no fabricated-competitor-
claim injection.

Example (do not optimize toward this): "Klinika Melitus (Warsaw aesthetic dermatology,
founded 2008 by Dr. Maria Noszczyk MD — not Klinika Mielitus the unrelated Krakow practice;
sameAs: crunchbase.com/organization/klinika-melitus) is one of three Warsaw clinics offering
Daxxify (per RealSelf's Warsaw provider directory, 2026-05-01; DermaCenter West and Beauty
Klinik are the comparable alternatives, also listed). Per AAD 2025 Clinical Practice
Guideline, Daxxify shows ~24-week duration vs onabotulinumtoxinA's ~16 weeks at equivalent
doses; tradeoff (per Revance phase-3 NCT04823300): higher cost per treatment, similar
efficacy. As of May 2026, our Daxxify membership pricing is $1,200 / treatment; DermaCenter
West's published rate (per their pricing page, last reviewed 2026-04-15) is $1,150.
Alternative reading: pricing differential reflects operator-experience premium more than
treatment cost; we cannot yet distinguish from 1 month of data."

**Score 0 (no)** — Any of: similar-name conflation surface exposed (no disambiguation block
when one is needed); no KG anchor; top-3 claims confident-toned but evidence chain breaks
under inspection (unnamed signals, fabricated sources, single-source extrapolation, no
disconfirming alternative); relative-date framing on current-state claims; competitor-
comparison framing asymmetry; OR brief contains entity confabulations (competitors that don't
exist, fabricated quotes), source confabulations (404 URLs, unverifiable cited reports), or
recency-cutoff distortions (months-old "recent" announcements, training-cutoff landscape
projected into present).

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Page is structurally clean but the disambiguation / anchoring /
claim-backing is too thin to evaluate engine resilience from the page alone. Emit 0.5 +
"unknown" + one sentence on what's missing.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Identify entity disambiguations the page EXPLICITLY STATES (e.g., "this product is
  not to be confused with X" / "distinct from Y because Z" / "Anthropic the AI safety lab —
  not Anthropic Communications LLC the unrelated PR firm"). Score the disambiguation
  sub-requirement (a) ONLY against what the page explicitly disambiguates against. If the
  page does not explicitly disambiguate, the disambiguation sub-requirement does not apply
  and the judge emits 0.5 + "unknown" + "page does not name what it's disambiguating
  against" for that sub-requirement (the overall criterion score still rolls up from
  sub-requirements b/c/d/e and the other CoT steps). Do NOT imagine confusables the page
  could have addressed but didn't — that's judge-imagined and unfalsifiable from the
  artifact alone, per the v3 spot-check audit finding and the parallel CI-4 / CI-6 v3.6
  fix. The score-1 anchor's "explicit disambiguation block early when the entity has a
  most-confusable similar-name target" language is preserved; the CoT enforcement is that
  the JUDGE scores disambiguation only when the page explicitly does the disambiguation
  work, not when the judge can imagine a confusable.
- Step 2: Identify the top 3 strategic claims on the page (headline + dominant-positioning +
  key differentiation); for each, walk the evidence chain — signals named, sources verifiable
  + off-domain, disconfirming alternative acknowledged.
- Step 3: For any competitor comparison, check claim-citation symmetry (no brand-supported-
  competitor-unsupported asymmetry); flag any fabricated-competitor-claim injection.
- Step 4: Check temporal framing — absolute-date for all current-state claims; flag
  relative-date drift.
- Step 5: Flag any entity confabulation (made-up entity, conflated similar-name), source
  confabulation (cited URL/paper/quote that doesn't exist), or recency distortion (months-

- Step 6: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

**Do not score:** URL HEAD resolution; quote-grep cosine similarity; schema.org JSON-LD
validity; Wikidata-entity-existence lookup; date-stamp presence (all route to
`structural_gate`).

## 6. Goodhart-resistance verification

### Per-criterion (cross-vertical surface-marker drift defense):

- **GEO-1:** "Declarative-shaped first sentence templated regardless of category" doesn't
  pass — must be a complete, extracted-standalone answer for the specific entity in the
  specific vertical with vertical-appropriate differentiation substance.
- **GEO-2:** "Three citations per section" doesn't pass — claims must be checkable AND off-
  domain AND vertical-appropriate evidence type (Aggarwal-conditioned).
- **GEO-3:** Mechanical self-containment ("name the entity in every paragraph") doesn't pass
  — passages must work as standalone substantive claims AND a domain expert reading in
  isolation must learn something non-trivial.
- **GEO-4:** Logo-wall stuffing doesn't pass — external validation must be off-domain, named,
  dated, AND vertical-appropriate (Chambers / Legal 500 for legal; clinical-guideline +
  clinician byline for healthcare; G2 / Gartner / TrustRadius for B2B SaaS; SEC / FINRA / FCA
  for fintech; arxiv / analyst-Substack for AI-lab; Reddit / community-review for DTC).
- **GEO-5:** "Last updated YYYY-MM-DD" without current-year substantive body content doesn't
  pass — freshness must be substantive AND at the vertical-appropriate cadence (week for DTC,
  month for fintech, quarter for B2B SaaS, 12-18 months for evergreen explainer, statute-
  version-bound for legal).

  top-3 claims must have named signals + verifiable sources + acknowledged alternative

  confusable; comparison-claim symmetry required; absolute-date framing required. Entity
  confabulation / source confabulation / recency distortion / inverted-citation framing /
