# GEO v2 spot-check

## §1 Reader spec verdict

**ALIGNED, with a residual concern that mirrors the CI v3.5 critique.** The reader spec correctly identifies the dual-audience pattern (founder/head of marketing/head of strategy + AI engine itself) and broadens the substitute-readers list from "Klinika + DWF + AI labs" to a US-primary SaaS / AI-lab / agency / service-business / finance / e-commerce mix. The 2018→2026 pivot framing — "they have been pitched enough SEO-pivoted-to-AEO content to recognize slot-fills" — is load-bearing because it discriminates against the deprecated playbook that §3 catalogues. The Aggarwal-method-by-domain anchoring ("Single-formula GEO is wrong by construction") is real epistemological work.

Residual concerns. **(a) The service-business / local owner-operator persona is the same out-of-family inclusion CI v3.5 carried.** "Founder or head of research at an AI lab (Anthropic / Cursor / Perplexity)" and "Owner-operator at a service business (medical / dental / aesthetic / legal / accounting / financial-planning / hospitality / specialty trades)" sit next to each other but commission GEO for fundamentally different reasons (local-pack capture + GBP NAP consistency vs. category-defining AI-citation share against named global competitors), at radically different scales (single-location Warsaw clinic vs. multi-region AI lab), and against radically different evidence substrates (RealSelf + Healthgrades + Polish Wikipedia vs. arxiv + Latent Space + Stratechery). The CI peer review flagged this; GEO inherits and arguably amplifies it because the §C/§E/§G section emphasis required for Klinika is nearly disjoint from what Anthropic needs. (b) **"Smart, time-poor, and skeptical" is decorative here too** — every 2026 GEO reader is time-poor and skeptical; the load-bearing differentiator is "they have been pitched enough SEO-pivoted-to-AEO content to recognize slot-fills" which already does the discriminating work.

## §1.5 Artifact shape verdict

**MISALIGNED — same load-bearing concern as CI v3.5, possibly more acute.** The 12-section §A-§L comprehensive GEO Strategic Plan (12,000-18,000 words across §A audit / §B cut-add / §C page-level / §D site-architecture / §E off-page / §F comparison-page / §G distribution / §H retrieval-testing / §I bio engineering / §J negative-AEO defense / §K 30/60/90 / §L appendices) is **research-derived without a single named client validation point**. Per `docs/research/2026-05-19-geo-comprehensive-scope.md` §5 ("Why ONE artifact, not 12"), the justification rests on (1) loop coherence, (2) single rubric, (3) CI precedent, (4) reader workflow ("a founder reading the plan reads it end-to-end once") — none of which cite a client who has actually requested or used a 12,000-18,000-word multi-component AEO strategic plan.

Four specific concerns the CI peer review applies to here, plus two GEO-specific ones:

1. **No client demand evidence.** §7 of the comprehensive-scope research lists Profound, Wordlift, Kalicube, Animalz, Backlinko, Lexicon Legal Content as SOTA exemplars — none ship the 12-section §A-§L bundle. Profound publishes individual blog posts. Wordlift sells a schema product. Lexicon Legal Content's "Generative Engine Optimization Strategy Guide for Law Firms" is the closest cited template and the spec does not say how long it is, what shape it takes, or whether any law firm actually executed against it. The "modular package is right" assertion is unsupported by client behavior.

2. **The architectural trick may be solving the wrong problem.** v1 (single landing page in one of 10 form factors) was scoped narrowly. v2 expands to "22 distinct GEO-relevant activity axes across 6 layers" and bundles them into one artifact. The 22-axis claim is real research; the *bundling decision* is internal organizational neatness ("loop coherence" + "single rubric") not reader-driven. Per CI v3.5 reasoning: clients commission GEO to *commit budget and engineering capacity to a prescription*, not to *receive a 12-section document*. Component §C + §B + §K (the page-level work + the cut/add + the 30/60/90) is what gets committed against. §D-§J reads as evidence the workflow CAN produce on demand.

3. **Section-shape bifurcation hidden behind "school B routing."** Production-default is all 12 sections; sibling-fork triggers (§F → comparison-page lane after 3+ clients; §E → off-page-signal-engineering lane after 3+ clients) mean the lane produces multiple artifact shapes depending on which sibling triggers have fired. The spec does not specify what the §A-§L plan looks like *after* a sibling-fork — does the GEO lane stop producing §E, or does it still produce a thin §E that points at the sibling lane? Same question for §F. This is exactly the decision-routing ambiguity CI v3.5 flagged.

4. **Per-component size envelopes look right in isolation.** §A 1,500-2,500w, §C 1,500-2,500w, §K 600-1,000w are all defensible individually. The *bundle* envelope (12,000-18,000w total, 20,000w ceiling enforced by `structural_gate`) is what's miscalibrated. A "smart, time-poor, skeptical" founder reading 18,000 words is reading §C + §K + skim of §B. §D, §H, §J are read once if at all and almost never used to commit action. The lane is producing §D-§J for organizational appearance, not reader value.

5. **GEO-specific: the comprehensive-scope research §6 acknowledges its own Goodhart risks (section-rotation slot-fill, long-document length bloat, section-decoupling) but the v2 spec carries those risks forward without variance-instrumentation TELEMETRY described.** Per design-guide §11.5, variance per criterion per generation is the only Goodhart-time-constant signal. §8 question 14 mentions variance instrumentation generically but doesn't tie it to *per-section* drift — if §E's structural_gate passes 100% generations 1-5 and 60% generation 6, the spec doesn't say how that gets caught. Per-section variance is the load-bearing telemetry, not per-criterion.

6. **GEO-specific: the §I bio architecture is doing double duty.** §I is named "load-bearing E-E-A-T signal in 2026" and is one of THREE sections the judge actually scopes (§A + §C + §I). But §I is only 400-800 words. So the judge core component is mostly §C (1,500-2,500w of page-level recommendations + sample sections) with §A audit findings and a brief §I bio spec — and §I gets scored by the same 6 criteria designed for page-level sample passages. GEO-1 (BLUF in first 40-75w) on a bio architecture spec is a category error. GEO-3 (passage self-containment) on a per-author Person-schema sameAs anchor list reads strange. The spec does not say whether the judge applies all 6 criteria to §I or just GEO-4 (entity stability + third-party validation).

**Recommended posture:** ship §C as production-default (with §B cut/add prescription as the framing); treat §A audit, §K roadmap, §I bio architecture as production-default thin sections; treat §D, §E, §F, §G, §H, §J, §L as on-demand evidence the workflow CAN produce when challenged but NOT as production-default deliverables until one real client has been observed using them to defend a decision. Same posture CI v3.5 needs.

## §4 Criteria spot-check (per criterion)

### GEO-1 — Answer-first BLUF compliance

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD.** "First 40-75 words" + "declarative-document register" + "no interrogative opener / no brand storytelling preamble / no deprecated keyword-density-targeted lede" + "substantive claim a domain expert in the vertical would defend" + "surgical-content-injection test" all carry. Three vertical examples (legal Section 230, B2B SaaS Linear, healthcare Klinika) discriminate well — the Klinika example carries first-cohort baggage (and that's the load-bearing test) but the legal/SaaS examples generalize.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** "What is X?" interrogative + brand storytelling + vague positioning + "templated answer with vertical-specific terms swapped in that wouldn't survive a domain-expert read" + explicit CUT 1 reference is concrete.
- CoT: **GOOD.** Step 3 (substance survives domain-expert read) is load-bearing.

### GEO-2 — Evidence density

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD with DRIFT.** "At least 3 specific claims paired with verifiable evidence" + "off-domain / first-party-data-anchored" + "Aggarwal domain-conditioned" + competitor-acknowledgment fold from v1 surgical restoration all carry. The "$249/month for 2,000 tracked keywords" vs "affordable plans for every budget" pair is JR-iterated and load-bearing. **However:** the "off-domain attribution also includes competitor wins where the client genuinely loses on a dimension" sub-clause is theoretical — the spec needs a 4th example showing a score-1 page that acknowledges a competitor genuinely winning. As written, the rejection-of-self-preference path could let the workflow score 1 without doing positive analytical work, same as CI v3.5's CI-3 concern.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** Self-citation farm + un-named "industry experts" + wrong-vertical evidence (healthcare citing B2B-SaaS aggregators) are concrete.
- CoT: **GOOD.** Step 2 sibling-domain-as-failing flag is enforceable.

### GEO-3 — Passage self-containment

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD.** "At least 3 substantive passages" + AND-conjunction (mechanical standalone AND substantive content) + "a domain expert reading the extracted passage learns a non-trivial claim, not a repeated definition" is the load-bearing language. Three vertical examples (SHIELD Act / Claude 4.7 specs / cast-iron-skillet) cover different evidence types.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** "Mechanically self-contained via entity-repetition but substantively empty" + explicit Goodhart "exactly-40-word answer-bait passage" reference catches the slot-fill mode named in §3d.
- CoT: **GOOD.**

### GEO-4 — Entity stability and third-party validation

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD with DRIFT.** Canonical KG anchor tiered (Wikidata > Crunchbase > LinkedIn > GBP > registry .gov) handles the Klinika SMB case correctly per §8 question 7. Per-vertical validation types are concrete. **However:** the "citability moat" sub-clause — "proprietary methodology, unique knowledge, category-specific technical depth" counts as third-party-equivalent validation because "no off-domain source can reproduce them" — is the same theoretical-rejection-of-anti-pattern concern as GEO-2. As written, this lets a vendor-vacuum page score 1 on GEO-4 by claiming "we have a proprietary methodology no one else can validate." A 4th example showing a score-1 page that earns the moat without falling into vendor-vacuum framing is needed. The §I bio architecture cross-check (Step 4 of CoT) only fires "where present in the fixture" — if §I is absent or templated, GEO-4 has no fallback.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** Logo-wall stuffing + self-comparison + sibling-domain-only validation + vendor-vacuum framing + vertical-mismatched external validation are concrete failure modes.
- CoT: **GOOD.** 5 steps is heavier than the others; load-bearing because §I has to be cross-checked.

### GEO-5 — Format-intent match and vertical-conditioned freshness

- Score-1 anchor: **GOOD.** Per-vertical freshness cadences (DTC week / fintech month / B2B SaaS quarter / healthcare 24mo on stable + 90d on emerging / legal 12mo statute-version-bound / AI-lab per-release / evergreen 12-18mo) are research-grounded and discriminating. "21 of 22 images lack alt text" specificity-discipline fold from v1 carries. Intent-mismatch test ("how much does Ahrefs cost" must surface pricing in first substantive passage, not company history) is load-bearing.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD with one concern.** "Last updated YYYY-MM-DD" current-year stamp on body content with no current-year references catches the §3d Goodhart "date-stamp gaming" mode. The score-0 anchor folds in "OR when the page recommendation carries technical guidance, it is vague boilerplate rather than concrete counts and named specifics" — this is GEO-5 doing GEO-2-like work and risks correlation. Likely candidate for the §8 question 1 redundancy check beyond the already-flagged GEO-1↔GEO-3 and GEO-2↔GEO-6 pairs.
- CoT: **GOOD.** Step 4 (substantive vs stamped-only freshness) catches the gaming mode.

### GEO-6 — Evidence chain survives engine-side re-citation

- Score-1 anchor: **DRIFT — risk of false-positive Goodhart from theatrical anti-pattern inclusion.** The 5 sub-requirements (disambiguation block + KG anchor + top-3 claims with named-signals-verifiable-sources-acknowledged-alternatives + absolute-date framing + comparison-claim symmetry) are research-grounded against the 5 named AI-failure surfaces. **However:** sub-requirement (a) "explicit disambiguation block early when the entity has a most-confusable similar-name target" is unfalsifiable from the artifact alone — the judge must imagine the similar-name confusable, then check if the page disambiguates against it. Same double-imagination concern CI v3.5 caught with CI-4 ("Identify the company's apparent priors from the brief's framing — but the brief is written FOR an unknown reader at an unknown company with unknown priors"). The Klinika example does the disambiguation work explicitly ("Klinika Melitus … not Klinika Mielitus the unrelated Krakow practice") but most fixtures will not have a known confusable, and the judge will hallucinate one to score against.
- Score-0 anchor: **GOOD.** "Top-3 claims confident-toned but evidence chain breaks under inspection" is concrete. Entity confabulation / source confabulation / recency distortion / inverted-citation framing / competitive-injection each forcing score 0 is enforceable from the artifact.
- CoT: **WRONG at Step 1, GOOD at 2-6.** Step 1 ("Identify the entity's most-confusable similar-name target from page context") cannot be reliably executed from a reference-free brief. Suggest rewrite: "Step 1: Check whether the brief itself names a disambiguation target (entity name + similar-name confusable). Only score against disambiguation when the brief either names the confusable explicitly OR when the entity has a well-known same-name conflict that any LLM-vintage judge would recognize."
- **Cross-criterion concern preserved from v1:** GEO-2 (evidence density) and GEO-6 (evidence chain) are likely to correlate >0.7 in the redundancy check — both test for off-domain attribution and acknowledged alternatives. §8 question 1 already flags this with "Most-likely-to-merge: GEO-2 ↔ GEO-6 (CI parallel suggests this may absorb)." The spec is honest about the absorption likelihood. If GEO-6 absorbs into GEO-2, the disambiguation + KG-anchor + absolute-date sub-requirements need to be re-folded — not lost.

## §8 Open questions completeness

3. **Does §D structural_gate-driven recommendation actually reach the site_engine lane?** §8 question 4 acknowledges the boundary needs adjudication "when site_engine v1 spec lands" but the spec ships §D as production-default. Same orphan concern as CI v3.5's Component E watchlist for MON. Either §D moves to optional until site_engine is implementation-ready, or the spec acknowledges the orphan. 4. **First-cohort overfitting at the SECTION level is unaddressed.** §8 question 9 ("First-cohort overfit re-validation triggers") talks about new-vertical fixtures. §8 question 12 acknowledges the trigger applies to "any new axis being added." ## Top 3 risks if shipped as-is

1. **Multi-component architecture ships → workflow optimizes for section count + structural_gate compliance, not actual reader-action-on-prescription.** Goodhart attack-surface is named in `docs/research/2026-05-19-geo-comprehensive-scope.md` §6 (section-rotation slot-fill, long-document length bloat, section-decoupling) but variance-instrumentation TELEMETRY at the per-section layer is not specified in §8 question 14. The lane will produce §A + §C scored against 6 outcome criteria + §B/§D/§E/§F/§G/§H/§J/§K/§L passing structural_gate, with no per-section drift signal. By generation 6-10, the workflow learns to template-recite §E (full Reddit + Wikipedia + Quora + community map verbatim) and §F (3 generic comparison targets without framing rationale) — exactly the §3d Goodhart modes the spec names but doesn't instrument for. **Mitigation: defer §D-§J until per-section variance instrumentation is shipped AND JR has run one cost-instrumented fixture end-to-end (same posture CI v3.5 needs).**

2. **§1.5 12-section plan ships → real clients receive 12,000-18,000 pages → read §B + §C + §K (≈ 3,000-4,500 words) → judge confirms §C is good (the only judge-scoped section that drives reader action) → loop converges on §C quality → §D-§J atrophy into Goodhart-attack vectors for sections no human reads.** This is the same failure mode as Phase-4 (workflow optimizes a surface marker disconnected from reader value), at the deliverable-package layer instead of the criterion-prose layer. The lane learns to produce structurally-compliant §D-§J that pass structural_gate and that no human ever opens. **Mitigation: ship §A + §B + §C + §K as production-default; §D + §E + §F + §G + §H + §I + §J + §L as optional deliverables instrumented for actual client-side open-rate before promoting to default.**

3. **GEO-6 score-1 anchor's "explicit disambiguation block when entity has a most-confusable similar-name target" CoT Step 1 ships → judge variance per generation becomes the highest of any criterion → GEO-6 gets flagged for redesign per design-guide §11.5 → the spec already predicts GEO-6 absorbs into GEO-2 in the redundancy check → BUT absorption happens before the disambiguation + KG-anchor + absolute-date sub-requirements get re-folded → the lane loses the AI-failure-surface coverage that justified the ≤5-ceiling exception in the first place.** Same shape as CI v3.5's CI-4 risk: the wrong criterion gets dropped (GEO-6 instead of pruning the GEO-1↔GEO-3 passage-structure redundancy or the GEO-2↔GEO-5 freshness-specificity correlation that GEO-5 score-0 anchor introduces). **Mitigation: rewrite GEO-6 CoT Step 1 to score on brief-stated disambiguation targets only (the brief either names the confusable explicitly OR the entity has a well-known same-name conflict any LLM judge would recognize). Then run the redundancy check, and let the absorption fall where it falls — but BEFORE the absorption fires, fold the 5 sub-requirements explicitly into GEO-2's score-1 anchor so they survive the merge.**

## Overall verdict

**NEEDS-EDIT.** Same shape as CI v3.5.

The judge layer (GEO-1 through GEO-6) is largely in good shape — v1 surgical restoration is preserved across 8 prose-folds, anchors are concrete with per-vertical examples, AND-conjunction discipline is explicit, Goodhart-modes are named per-criterion + per-vertical + per-§A-§L-envelope. **Three judge-layer edits before ship:** (a) rewrite GEO-6 CoT Step 1 to score against brief-stated disambiguation targets, not judge-imagined similar-name confusables; (b) add a 4th GEO-2 example demonstrating a score-1 page that acknowledges a competitor genuinely winning, AND a 4th GEO-4 example demonstrating a score-1 "citability moat" that doesn't fall into vendor-vacuum framing; (c) tighten GEO-5 score-0's "concrete counts vs vague boilerplate" sub-clause to specify that the specificity discipline is judge-readable in body content only, not via structural_gate cross-reference — the current phrasing risks GEO-5↔GEO-2 redundancy beyond the already-flagged GEO-1↔GEO-3 and GEO-2↔GEO-6 pairs.

The §1.5 multi-component package architecture is the load-bearing concern. It is research-derived without a single client validation point. It solves a problem (comprehensive 22-axis coverage) that is real, but bundles the solution into a 12,000-18,000-word artifact for organizational/loop-coherence reasons that have not been validated against any client reading behavior. **The recommendation is to ship §C + §B + §K + thin §A + thin §I as production-default** (the page-level work, the cut/add prescription, the 30/60/90 with named owners, the audit findings on the page-level axes, and the bio architecture as the load-bearing E-E-A-T signal); treat §D, §E, §F, §G, §H, §J, §L as on-demand evidence the workflow CAN produce when challenged, and revisit promotion to production-default after one real client has been observed using §D-§J to defend a decision. The "judge scopes Page-level core component; structural_gate validates §B-§L outer envelope; rubric architecture unchanged" framing is architecturally elegant but operationally inverted — the workflow burns evolution generations producing §D-§J that no reader uses, then those sections become Goodhart-attack vectors when the workflow learns the structural_gate's word-count + sub-section-presence shape.

The §8 open-questions list is comprehensive on internal-engineering risks (redundancy check, sibling-fork triggers, fixture coverage, multi-component fixture validation, variance instrumentation, propagation to other lanes) but thin on reader-validation risks. The most important missing question (mirroring CI v3.5): *what evidence do we have that any real client wants the 12-section §A-§L bundle shape?* If the honest answer is "none — it's research-derived from 22-axis coverage + CI artifact-shape precedent," that's a reason to gate-flip §D-§J to optional before ship.

The modern-lever bias (penalize old SEO / reward modern AEO) is **correctly load-bearing.** The 20-cut + 15-add catalogue is research-grounded (Aggarwal KDD 2024 + Ahrefs studies + ALM Corp + Frase 2026 + Google March/April/May 2026 algorithm updates). It earns its place. The first-cohort overfitting watch (US-primary SaaS/AI/agency/service/finance/e-commerce vs DWF/Klinika/Anthropic/Perplexity bias) is documented as a watch trigger at §8 question 9 — but the section-level overfitting risk (per-vertical community-substrate map in §E, per-vertical evidence-substrate in §A, per-language locale-flex throughout) is unaddressed. The GEO ↔ site_engine boundary at §8 question 4 is **acknowledged but not explicit** — the spec says "GEO produces recommendations in §D; site_engine implements pages" but does not specify the topology-spec contract format the two lanes share, and memory notes site_engine is in mid-build, so the boundary draws against a moving target.

Two specific gates before ship: (a) decide whether GEO-6 absorbs into GEO-2 explicitly *before* the redundancy check fires, or commit to running the redundancy check first and accepting the absorption. The current spec hedges both directions ("subject to the same redundancy check … don't fight the absorption when it happens") which means the AI-failure-surface coverage is at risk of falling through the merge. (b) decide whether §D-§J ship as production-default or as on-demand, and document the decision criterion. The current spec ships them all production-default and gates only sibling-forks (§F → comparison-page lane; §E → off-page-signal lane) on 3+ clients with weekly/monthly cadence — but the production-default-vs-optional split is the load-bearing question, not the sibling-fork-vs-stay-inside-GEO question.
