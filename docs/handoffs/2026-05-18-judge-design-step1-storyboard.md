# Storyboard — Optimal-Output Spec (DRAFT v2)

Conforms to `docs/rubrics/judge-design-guide.md` with one documented exception (§7). Frameworks (MrBeast handbook, Pixar 22 Rules, Save the Cat, Casey Neistat structure, Johnny Harris visual-first method, Hank/John Green imagine-others-complexly, Hormozi Hook-Retain-Reward, Story Spine, Dan Harmon Story Circle) inform the spec's reader/success/failure framing and are the judge's reasoning toolkit. They do NOT appear by name in criterion prose.

This v2 supersedes v1 by widening the **lane scope** — not the judge scope — from "5 short video story plans" to a comprehensive four-part video program (Strategy / Production / Distribution & community / Coaching & growth) per `docs/research/2026-05-19-storyboard-comprehensive-scope.md`. The judge continues to score the 5-plan portfolio (Part 2 — Production); program-level components in Parts 1, 3, 4 are validated by `structural_gate` for presence and conformance. This is the School B decision recorded 2026-05-19: keep the judge layer atomic on the 5-plan portfolio (the unit of irreducible creative bet) while letting `structural_gate` enforce that the surrounding video program ships.

The widening matters because a 2026 AI-native agency that hands a client "5 short video story plans" has shipped roughly 10% of what their video presence needs. Distribution is the production constraint, not the production output. Series arc — not single video — is the unit of audience growth. Thumbnail and title strategy drive 30–60% of CTR variance. Comment management is a real workflow. Founder-led on-camera with AI augmentation beats AI-only generation across every B2B vertical gofreddy serves. None of these are judge-scoreable as outcome questions on a 5-plan portfolio; all of them are structurally testable as program-level components.

The four research deliverables underpinning v1 (voice fidelity 70–80% PAN-CLEF ceiling, AI failure modes 40–80% open-ended confab, cold-start regression-to-mean, capability fleet rotation) stay load-bearing for SB-1..SB-6. The new v2 research deliverable (`2026-05-19-storyboard-comprehensive-scope.md`) is load-bearing for the program layer — §1.5 artifact-shape, §3 modern-lever bias, §1 broadened substitute readers, §8 sibling-fork triggers.

The v0 skeleton compressed 10 candidate criteria into 5. v1 KEPT the 5-criterion spine and added a 6th as a documented exception per the ≤5-ceiling rule (the AI-failure surface SB-1..SB-5 cannot catch is measurable and broadcast-risk-asymmetric — a storyboard fabrication ships to thousands; a CI brief fabrication misleads one decision-maker). v2 holds at 6 criteria. Expanding the lane scope does not justify expanding the judge layer; the program-level surfaces ride structural_gate.

## 1. Reader (LOCKED 2026-05-19)

A founder, partner, or content-program owner at a tech-savvy company that has commissioned a video program to drive visibility / authority / lead-gen. They have 30–60 minutes to read the program deliverable (one sitting), and within that, **5–15 minutes** specifically to evaluate the 5-plan portfolio in Part 2 — they will commit to producing one of 5 plans, or reject all 5 and ask for a new set. The 5-plan portfolio is what the judge scores. The rest of the program (Parts 1, 3, 4) frames the bet — strategy positions the bet, distribution productionizes it across platforms, coaching de-risks the founder's on-camera execution.

A plan in the Part-2 portfolio is committable when it (a) sounds like THEM rather than a generic AI imitation, (b) has a hook they could honestly text a friend ("you have to see this"), (c) earns its claimed emotional arc through specific producing beats not just metadata declarations, (d) lands within the declared rendering model's envelope for the bits they'll generate AND within their own production-source constraints (location, rights, guest access), (e) reads as 5 distinct bets rather than 5 variations on the safest premise, (f) survives source-tracing — every stat / quote / experiential claim either cites a source, matches pattern-data, or is explicitly flagged scripted-fiction.

**Decision-making shape varies by client archetype** (per cold-start research §3 + comprehensive-scope research §4.7):

- **Creator-led** — solo content creator with established voice signature (a YouTube creator, a TikTok personality, a podcast host). Pattern data is rich; the reader IS the voice authority; the brand-voice doc, if it exists, is descriptive of an observed practice. **Stretch case for gofreddy** — most clients are not creator-led.
- **Brand-author** — a creator strategist commissioning storyboards for a brand client, or an in-house content lead at a media-native brand. Pattern data is the brand's editorial voice; the brief specifies what the brand wants to sound like; the reader is responsible to the brand's voice authority, not their own.
- **Founder-led business** — a founder-CEO or partner whose video content represents the company (a SaaS founder doing thought-leadership; an AI-lab researcher doing demo-led content; an agency principal doing craft-led commentary; a law-firm partner explaining a regulatory shift; a finance operator running weekly market commentary; an e-commerce founder running product-demo-led short-form). **This is the dominant gofreddy case.** Pattern data is the founder's published written corpus + any podcast appearances + any prior video. On-camera voice is partly emergent because they haven't shot much yet.

The plan still has to drive concrete production-commitment regardless of which archetype the reader operates in — but the voice-fidelity anchor (SB-1) differs across archetypes (observed voice for creator-led; brand editorial voice for brand-author; founder's published anchor for founder-led).

**Reading time is not load-bearing.** They read until they have what they need, then stop. Length guidelines route to `structural_gate`, not the judge.

**Substitute readers — US-primary, broadened beyond first-cohort.** The same program deliverable should also serve: a SaaS founder running founder-led thought-leadership inside a 50–500-employee company; an AI-lab researcher / founder building a personal channel adjacent to their lab's work; an agency principal at a 10–50-person craft shop running founder-led visibility; a service-firm partner (law / consulting / accountancy / wealth management) running regulatory-or-thesis commentary; a fintech operator running market-moment commentary; an e-commerce operator running product-demo-led short-form. Klinika (Polish aesthetic dermatology) and DWF (Polish legal services) are concrete first-cohort fixtures, **not** architectural target. **gofreddy serves business clients, not YouTube creators primarily.** When the spec uses MrBeast / Casey Neistat / Cleo Abram / Hank Green / Tom Scott as anchors, they are concrete reference points for the judge's reasoning toolkit, not the population gofreddy is selling to.

**First-cohort overfit watch — reinforced 2026-05-19 v3.** Current SB lane fixtures are BUSINESS clients (Klinika, DWF, B2B SaaS founder) — NOT established YouTube creators with rich pattern data. SB-1 prose must cover "the client's published anchor" (written essays, LinkedIn posts, podcast transcripts, founder appearances) NOT only "the creator's voice family" (which presumes a YouTube/TikTok-shaped catalog). When client #5+ onboards from an established-creator archetype, re-validate SB-1 prose (see §8). **The framing this spec defends:** SB serves BUSINESS clients first; YouTube creators are a stretch case, not the dominant population. The Polish-market first-cohort (Klinika PL, DWF PL) is concrete fixture, not architectural target — US-primary is the architectural default, and the Polish + US-primary tension is absorbed by treating PL fixtures as anchor-data and US-primary as the design center. Re-validation of SB-1 archetype examples should run across 5+ verticals (SaaS / AI lab / agency / service firm / finance / e-commerce) BEFORE a 4th-archetype client onboards, not after — pre-rotation of examples mitigates the slot-fill pathology where workflow-under-selection-pressure converges on whatever shape scores well on the first 3 fixtures.

NOT the reader: a VFX supervisor finalizing shot specs; a screenwriter writing for traditional film; a marketing team using a story plan as a sales deck; a post-production editor doing color and grade; a YouTube creator who does this for a living and wants 5 finished scripts rather than 5 bets.

## 1.5. Artifact shape (LOCKED 2026-05-19 — multi-component deliverable, judge atomic at Part 2)

**The lane produces a four-part comprehensive video program.** Part 2 (the 5-plan portfolio) is the judge's scope. Parts 1, 3, 4 are validated by `structural_gate` for component presence and conformance. The lane unit is the program; the judge unit is the Part-2 portfolio.

### 1.5.1 Part 1 — STRATEGY (Component A) — structural_gate-validated

The macro layer the production rests on. Required components:

- **Channel positioning + voice plan** — single-source-of-truth voice document derived from founder's published anchor; reader avatar specification; brand signature (caption style, thumbnail aesthetic, music palette, color treatment).
- **Content pillars** — 3–5 pillars naming the editorial territory (e.g., for a SaaS founder: "shipping in production" / "team building lessons" / "industry frame-of-the-week" / "customer story extracts"); each pillar described with its purpose, target audience slice, and cadence.
- **Cadence plan** — per-pillar, per-platform publish rhythm with explicit weekly / monthly counts. Cadence must be defensible against staffing reality (a 1-person founder team cannot ship 5/wk long-form + daily Shorts; the cadence must say what's realistic).
- **Platform mix** — explicit YouTube long-form / Shorts / TikTok / IG Reels / LinkedIn video / X video / podcast-as-video declaration with primary / secondary / tertiary tier per platform. Vertical-aware (B2B founder defaults LinkedIn-primary; finance operator defaults YouTube-primary; e-commerce defaults TikTok-primary).

**Size envelope:** Part 1 ≈ 1,500–4,000 words depending on client complexity and pillar count. Refresh quarterly (not per-plan-batch).

### 1.5.2 Part 2 — PRODUCTION (Component B, judge core) — SB-1..SB-6 scope

**THE 5-PLAN PORTFOLIO IS THE JUDGE'S ATOMIC UNIT.** Five short-form video story plans, each 90 seconds to 8 minutes. This shape is locked because shape-drift Goodhart is a documented failure mode in evolution loops: under selection pressure, the workflow learns that single-plan outputs score higher on SB-2 / SB-3 (hook, emotional arc) while portfolio outputs score higher on SB-5 (diversity), producing inconsistent artifact shapes. The 5-plan set is the artifact unit, not the individual plan.

**Form factor per plan:**

- One **voice-script** (the spoken VO or on-camera dialogue) sized to the target plan duration
- One **scene list** with named beats, durations, camera-move primaries, consistency anchors
- One **declared rendering model** field (routes to `configs/storyboard/supported_models.yaml`)
- Plan-level metadata: hook sentence, claimed emotional arc, declared stakes, citation list (for any factual claim), pattern-data anchor references (for first-person claims)

**Additional Part-2 components (structural_gate-validated, not judge-scored):**

- **Thumbnail strategy + samples** — 2–4 thumbnail concept directions per plan (composition + facial expression + props + text + color palette). Optional generated mockups via Nano Banana Pro / Gemini Imagegen / GPT-4o image / Flux for client review.
- **Title strategy** — one title variant per thumbnail (paired). Title craft: front-loaded specificity, curiosity-gap-without-clickbait, contrastive framing, numeric anchoring, named-entity inclusion, throat-clearing removed.

**Out of scope shapes at the judge layer:**

- A single video plan (the judge unit is the 5-plan set)
- A traditional screenplay or shooting script (different audience, different format)
- A marketing deck or campaign storyboard (sells the idea to client; doesn't drive production commitment)
- Longer-form episodic content (>8 minutes per plan; serial structure handled by series-arc in Part 3)

**Size envelope per plan:** voice-script + scene-list + metadata typically 600–2,000 words per plan; thumbnail + title strategy ~200–400 words per plan. Five plans together: ~5,000–12,000 words.

### 1.5.3 Part 3 — DISTRIBUTION & COMMUNITY (Component C) — structural_gate-validated

The distribution layer that turns one long-form anchor into a multi-platform program. Required components per long-form plan in the Part-2 portfolio:

- **Cross-platform native cuts roster** — for each long-form plan, the explicit list of platform-native derivatives: YouTube main (16:9, full plan) + YouTube Shorts (9:16, hook-front, ≤60s) + TikTok (9:16, audio-first, 9–90s) + Instagram Reels (9:16, cover-frame-and-music-driven, 15–60s) + LinkedIn video (square or 9:16, captions-first for sound-off audience, ≤2:30) + X video (16:9 or 9:16, thread-friendly cuts, ≤2:20) + podcast video (16:9, long-form, light editing). Cuts are primary artifacts designed at plan stage, **not** post-hoc re-aspect-ratio extractions.
- **Comment management plan** — pinned comment per plan; first-30-min reply velocity target; comment-magnet engineering at script stage (unanswered question at end, controversial-but-defensible take, specific call for story from viewers); heart / pin / reply / engage workflow per video.
- **Series-arc plan** — 4–12 video commitment around a shared premise that compounds week over week. Components: premise frame across N videos; title-naming convention (Episode N: [hook]); thumbnail visual signature (same font / color / character treatment); inter-video callback structure (Ep 3 callbacks Ep 1); audience commitment ramp (Ep 1 introduces; Ep 5 deepens; Ep 12 pays off); measurement frame across the arc.
- **Cross-creator collaboration plan** — 3–5 named creators to approach for guest appearances, interview slots, cross-promos per quarter; outreach scripts; reciprocal-value offer (what you give them in exchange); specific episode / slot positioning. Founder-led clients benefit specifically from podcast-tour strategies.

**Size envelope:** Part 3 ≈ 1,500–4,000 words depending on series-arc depth and collab roster size. Refreshed monthly (cadence shorter than Part 1).

### 1.5.4 Part 4 — COACHING & GROWTH (Component D) — structural_gate-validated

The execution layer that converts a plan into shipped video. Required components:

- **On-camera coaching brief for founder** — posture / framing / lighting kit (typical first-month deliverables); voice projection / pace / breath drills (typical month-2); authenticity-vs-polish calibration (ongoing); energy management for multi-take days; specific scripts for repeat-shoot scenarios (founder Q&A intro, customer testimonial intro, etc.). Refresh quarterly per founder.
- **30/60/90 execution roadmap** — Days 1–30 (foundational: kit setup, first 2–3 plans shipped, first reps); Days 31–60 (cadence stabilization: weekly cadence locked, distribution playbook active); Days 61–90 (compounding: series-arc Ep 4+ shipping, collab pipeline active, measurement loop closing). Staffing and tooling implications named.
- **Measurement frame + retrospective cadence** — per-video metrics tracked (CTR, AVD, AVD-ratio, 30-sec hold, comment-velocity, like-velocity, share-velocity, subscriber-add per video, lead-gen attribution); series-arc retro at week 4 driving next-arc plan; quarterly content audit refresh.
- **Audience-building integration** — email-list CTAs per plan (end-card "subscribe to the newsletter"; pinned comment with newsletter link; lead-magnet specific to plan topic). Owned-channel conversion is the long-term moat.
- **Monetization integration** — per-plan monetization mode declared (lead-gen for B2B; sponsorship slot for creator-led; product-demo conversion for e-commerce). CTAs designed accordingly. For most gofreddy clients (B2B founder-led), lead-gen dominates; YouTube Partner ad revenue is small relative to lead-gen.

**Size envelope:** Part 4 ≈ 2,000–6,000 words (coaching brief drives most of this). 30/60/90 roadmap is the operational spine.

### 1.5.5 Why this split

**Substrate-readiness gate.** The 4-part comprehensive video program (Part 1 Strategy + Part 2 Production [5-plan portfolio = judge core] + Part 3 Distribution & Community + Part 4 Coaching & Growth) describes the COMPREHENSIVE workflow target. Part 2 (5-plan portfolio) ships at substrate-current — `session_eval_storyboard.py` reliably emits stories. Parts 1, 3, 4 ship as substrate emission catches up: Part 1 (strategy doc) when channel-positioning template generator exists in workflow; Part 3 (cross-platform native cuts + comment management + series-arc + collab) when per-platform repurposing tooling + comment-magnet engineering substrate exists; Part 4 (coaching brief + 30/60/90 + analytics + monetization) when client-onboarding substrate matures. Until each Part's substrate emits, structural-gate fails if v3 ships against the full 4-part bundle. Comprehensive scope is the SPEC TARGET; client-side shipping gated on substrate readiness. Operator note: SB v3 can ship to evolution with only Part 2 substrate-current; Parts 1, 3, 4 enter the structural_gate check set incrementally as their substrate matures, NOT as a single switch-flip.

The Reader spec (founder / partner / content-program owner with 30–60 minutes to absorb, 5–15 minutes specifically on the portfolio) and Success spec (commit to producing one Part-2 plan AND execute Parts 1, 3, 4 against the 30/60/90) point to program-as-deliverable. SB-5 portfolio diversity presumes 5 plans; SB-1 voice fidelity is scored per-plan but read across the portfolio for cohort-default detection. Parts 1, 3, 4 cannot be scored as outcome questions on a 5-plan portfolio — they are program-level shape claims that structural_gate can verify deterministically (cadence conforms to platform-mix; native cuts present per platform; series-arc has N episodes; coaching brief covers required surfaces; founder roadmap covers Days 1–90).

**Shape enforcement lives in `structural_gate`, NOT in the judge criteria.** Per design guide §11.1, the judge tests outcomes (SB-1..SB-6 below); the workflow's `structural_gate` tests shape conformance. v2 expansion means structural_gate now checks: 5 plans present in Part 2; voice-script and scene-list per plan; declared rendering model in supported list; citation list per plan; consistency-anchor section per plan; **plus** Part-1 strategy components present (positioning, voice plan, pillars, cadence, platform mix); **plus** Part-3 distribution components present per plan (native-cut roster covering YT main + 1+ short-form + LinkedIn for B2B + podcast for thought-leadership; comment-management plan present; series-arc plan with N≥4 episodes; collab roster with N≥3 named targets); **plus** Part-4 coaching components present (coaching brief, 30/60/90 roadmap, measurement frame, email-CTA per plan, monetization mode declared). Per design guide §11.1, this preserves the outcome-question-not-feature-check discipline at the judge layer while still defending against shape-drift at the program layer.

**Empirical validation scope.** Form factor is research-grounded against creator-strategist literature (MrBeast / Pixar / Neistat / Harris / Modern Wisdom / Cleo Abram / Lex Fridman / Logan Ury) + first-cohort business-client fixtures (Klinika, DWF, B2B SaaS founder). When fixtures from new archetypes appear — established YouTube creator with deep pattern data, podcaster commissioning video adaptation, music artist commissioning a video plan, e-commerce operator running TikTok-primary short-form — re-validate the form factor. Established creators may justify lane variants; founder-led may justify shorter portfolios (3 plans probing 3 postures while founder discovers voice). Sibling-fork triggers documented in §8.

## 2. Success — what the reader DOES (LOCKED 2026-05-19)

After reading the program deliverable, the reader:

1. **Commits to producing one specific plan from the Part-2 portfolio** — or rejects all 5 and asks for a new set. Commitment is specific enough that they could pitch the plan in 30 seconds to their editor and the editor would immediately know what to cut for. The hook is something they'd say aloud in a coffee meeting and the listener would ask "send me the link when it's done." The claimed emotional response — wonder, dread, awe, recognition, vulnerability — is something they could point at on the page.
2. **Knows what they're giving up** by picking this plan over the other four — they could explain to their producer in one sentence why this premise beats the next-best alternative.
3. **Sleep test passes** — slept on it overnight, would commit to producing the same plan tomorrow; the plan's promise survives 24 hours of reflection.
4. **Plan is traceable** — every factual claim has a citation; every first-person experiential claim either matches the creator's published anchor, is explicitly flagged scripted-fiction, or is generically lived-in. When the creator shoots it, the resulting video looks like the creator made it.
5. **Executes against the 30/60/90 roadmap (Part 4)** — sets up the lighting / mic / framing kit Days 1–30; locks weekly cadence Days 31–60; begins series-arc continuation Days 61–90. Roadmap is realistic for their staffing.
6. **Uses thumbnail + title strategy (Part 2)** — picks one of 2–4 thumbnail directions per shipped plan; tests title variants on Shorts soft-publish before committing to long-form publish.
7. **Implements cross-platform native cuts (Part 3)** — when the long-form ships, the 6+ native cuts roster ships within the same week. Distribution is not "fix it later"; it is designed in.
8. **Manages comments (Part 3)** — pins the prepared comment within 30 min of publish; replies to first 10–20 comments in first hour; uses comment-magnet engineering on script as planned.
9. **Founder follows coaching brief (Part 4)** — posture / framing / voice / breath drills happen weekly; quarterly coaching refresh runs against on-camera footage from prior quarter.
10. **Series-arc compounds (Part 3)** — Episode 1 → Episode 4 sees measurable subscriber-add velocity; Ep 5+ uses Ep 1–4 retro learnings; collab pipeline yields 1+ confirmed guest by Day 60.

World-class real-world exemplars — used as quality anchors, NOT as templates to copy. Two parallel exemplar sets: creator-ceiling (real exemplars of video-craft ceiling) and business-client-ceiling (real exemplars of founder-led / B2B video-craft ceiling). gofreddy sells to the second population; the first sets the upper bound on what video-craft can achieve.

**Cross-form rigor — creator-ceiling (the upper bound of video-craft):**
- **MrBeast handbook stair-step** — $1 firework → $100K firework → world-record firework. Progressive escalation as the within-video version of portfolio diversity. The handbook is explicit that wow-factor + retention curve do NOT save a plan with no stakes. Dedicated thumbnail team (15+ variants per video, A/B tested).
- **Johnny Harris visual-first script** — visual evidence carries the script; the voice-script supports what the eye is processing. Most rigorous of the reference set on script-vs-visual subordination. Geographic / geopolitical explainer.

**Cross-form rigor — business-client-ceiling (the upper bound of founder-led / B2B video-craft):**
- **Cody Schneider Doola walkthroughs** — founder-led on-camera SaaS demo walkthroughs that earn trust through specific feature-by-feature concrete demonstration; not theatrical, not "creator-shaped," but unmistakably high-craft for the founder-led B2B ceiling.
- **Lenny Rachitsky podcast video** — long-form operator interviews shot with production-grade lighting + camera + audio, plus systematic clip-extraction workflow. Highest-leverage cadence in the founder-led-thought-leadership category; voice-as-discipline + interview-as-research instrument.
- **Anthropic technical demos** — research-engineer-led product-demo video where the demonstrator's domain mastery carries the narrative; not influencer-shaped; ceiling for AI-lab founder vertical.
- **Stripe Sessions clips** — multi-day developer-conference talk-extraction with strong cinematography + intentional staging + clear narrative arc per clip; the ceiling for B2B-SaaS founder-led conference-derived content.
- **Mercury founder updates** — founder-led quarterly + monthly cadence updates with consistent on-camera presence, brand-disciplined visual system, and unbroken trust register; ceiling for the fintech founder-led category.

**Practitioner-grade (the achievable floor for founder-led):**
- **Casey Neistat three-act-in-ten-minutes** — three-act compression where the bookend reframes the opening. Workmanlike, closer to what real fixture plans look like.
- **Hank Green vlogbrothers** — written for one specific person (his brother). Voice-as-discipline grounded in a known audience rather than "the internet."
- **Tom Scott single-take** — pause-and-reveal beats calibrated; the camera does the work that cuts would do. Voice as cadence.
- **Cleo Abram portfolio** — "Huge If True" — shorts that share a creative universe but bet on 5 different premises. Series-arc-with-portfolio-diversity exemplar.
- **Modern Wisdom (Chris Williamson)** — weekly interview + clip-extraction workflow. Long-form interview plus 8–12 cut-extractions is among the highest-leverage cadences in 2026 for thought-leadership.
- **Lenny Rachitsky** — B2B SaaS founder content; podcast + newsletter + course portfolio; light video but high signal. Lesson: B2B founder content doesn't need video volume — high-quality long-form interviews + LinkedIn clips suffice.
- **Patrick Boyle** — weekly market commentary; dry-precise voice; lesson for finance vertical: market-moment cadence works.

What ties these together: a point of view at the top, structural reasoning about why this story exists, beats that earn their emotional position, a premise the creator could defend over a coffee meeting, distribution and comment-strategy designed at plan stage, series-arc that compounds rather than episodic-as-standalone.

## 3. Failure — mediocre, modern-lever-blind, and Goodhart-collapse (LOCKED 2026-05-19)

### 3a. Mediocre — failure modes the judge must discriminate against on the 5-plan portfolio

**Well-shot and pointless.** Plan moves competently, every beat lands on the page, the emotional metadata claims a coherent arc — and the story has nothing at stake. Reads as "a person walks into a coffee shop and has a realization" (creator-shape failure) OR "a SaaS founder records a polished 90-second monologue with cinematic b-roll and no actionable insight" (business-client-shape failure). The MrBeast handbook's named "well-shot, well-paced, pointless" failure is ONE reference instance; the failure mode is general — production-craft compliance with absent substance. Most common AI-generated short-form failure mode across both creator and business-client shapes.

**Generic creator voice — the median-creator trap.** The voice-script reads as a competent generic creator essay. No signature obsessions, no distinctive cadence, no named props the creator has actually used. Per stylometric research, frontier LLMs without explicit anchor converge on a "high-entropy YouTube essay neutral" register matching nobody. Resulting video would look like ChatGPT's idea of a creator brief.

**5 plans that are 5 variations on the safest premise.** Portfolio dressed to look diverse — different premises in surface description, different emotional registers in metadata — collapsing under inspection to the same structural shape. The AI found one safe premise and 5-times-served the same dish with different garnishes.

**Capability-blind ambition.** Plan specifies shots that current AI video models cannot render at usable quality (legible long text on signs, sustained multi-character lip-sync to externally-authored dialogue, multi-room continuous-motion takes, sub-second-precise timing of named beats, complex multi-constraint camera moves) — AND does NOT flag fallback strategy. The creator commits, regenerate-loops eat 20–50 credits, the artifact-laden gen ships into the final cut, or the team falls back mid-production to a salvage live-action shoot. Capability-blind plans waste real production money — industry-reported 15–25% credit-waste rate.

### 3b. Modern-lever-blind — 14 CUTS the program must NOT ship (program-level, validated by structural_gate at Parts 1/3/4)

Patterns a 2026 AI-native agency must not ship. Many are still default-mode for AI-generated video workflows. All are reasons clients eventually fire their content agency.

1. **Talking-head-only.** Founder reading a script directly to camera, no b-roll, no motion, no graphics. Worked in 2018; 2026 algorithm + audience both downrank. Talking-head is a *frame* (highest-trust), not a *format*.
2. **Generic AI-explainer with "Here's the thing" / "Stop X. Start Y." voiceover.** The Kapwing 278-AI-slop-channels analysis catalogued exactly this pattern (ElevenLabs default voice + Pexels stock + ChatGPT copy + "moreover/furthermore/in conclusion"). Bottom-tier CPM; reputation-destroying for B2B.
3. **Motivational montage with stock music.** Inspirational VO + Pexels stock + Epidemic uplift. Inflates production-value illusion for a week; degrades brand for a year.
4. **Hook-formula slot-fill across the portfolio.** All 5 plans open with "What if I told you…" OR "Stop doing X." OR "I tried X for 30 days." Cut at script stage (judge-scored under SB-5).
5. **5 plans, same premise, different garnish.** §3a mediocre mode 3 (judge-scored under SB-5).
6. **Plan with no thumbnail / title / distribution craft.** Stopping at script + scene list is a 2015 deliverable. No thumbnail = no CTR strategy. (Validated by Part-2 structural_gate.)
7. **One-video-fits-all-platforms.** Horizontal long-form chopped post-hoc into Shorts/Reels/TikTok yields the slop pattern. Each platform needs native design from plan stage. (Validated by Part-3 native-cuts structural_gate.)
8. **Cinematic AI-generated brand film with no founder presence.** Nike-style 60s entirely in Veo 4 + Runway. Looks impressive; converts near-zero for B2B. Founder absence kills trust in founder-led-business case.
9. **"Subscribe and ring the bell" CTAs.** Generic CTAs generate near-zero conversion. Specific CTAs work ("comment if you've ever shipped a 3am hotfix"; "join 8k SaaS founders on the newsletter"). (Validated by Part-4 monetization structural_gate.)
10. **Episode-as-standalone.** Video N without explicit relationship to N-1 and N+1 leaves audience-building on the table. (Validated by Part-3 series-arc structural_gate.)
11. **No measurement loop.** Open-loop content gets worse over time. (Validated by Part-4 measurement-frame structural_gate.)
12. **Generic-creator-voice in the founder's mouth.** The "high-entropy YouTube essay neutral" register with the founder's name attached. (Judge-scored under SB-1.)
13. **"5 video story plans" as the finished product.** A v1-shape deliverable, treated as the final, is itself a cut. 2026 demands the full video-program deliverable. (Validated by overall program-shape structural_gate — Parts 1, 3, 4 present.)
14. **Anti-platform tactics.** Horizontal content on TikTok without subtitles; LinkedIn >2:30 without strong hook; X video with no captions; YouTube without chapter markers. (Validated by Part-3 platform-native-cuts structural_gate.)

### 3d. Goodhart-collapse — Phase 4 pathology + storyboard-specific AI-failure surfaces (judge-layer)

**Phase 4 pathology (the historical Goodhart trap).** 50-generation evolution against a feature-checking judge produced exactly the pathology rolled back at `c76f051`. The workflow learns to slot-fill surface markers:

- **Every plan opens with a high-specificity sensory hook** regardless of whether the rest of the plan supports it. Surface marker present, premise empty.
- **Every plan declares a target emotion in metadata** ("the viewer now feels dread at 0:35") that isn't earned by any specific producing beat.
- **Every plan inserts a "stakes" sentence** whether the story has actual stakes or not. The sentence is the marker; the stakes are absent.
- **Every plan namechecks the creator's known obsessions** whether they're load-bearing or just lip-service. Pattern-data fragments stuck on without grounding.
- **Every set of 5 plans is templated to look diverse** — different premises labeled, different emotional registers labeled — while all 5 collapse to the same structural shape.

**Storyboard-specific AI-failure surfaces (per the four research deliverables):**

- **Script confabulation — fake stats, fake studies, fake quotes.** The plan hooks or pays off on a factual claim: "73% of creators…", "A Harvard 2024 study showed…", "As Einstein once said…". No cited source. Confabulation rates: 40–80% on open-ended generation; 19.9% citation-fab rate on GPT-4o literature reviews (Chelli et al. 2025); 75% case-fabrication on legal QA (Stanford 2024 RegLab). Catastrophic for storyboards specifically because the rendered video *broadcasts* the falsehood to thousands.

- **False personal experience — first-person "I" for events that didn't happen.** Plan generates first-person voice-script describing events the creator didn't experience: "I tried X for 30 days" when they didn't, "When I was in Tokyo last summer" when they weren't, "my mom always told me" — fabricated relational anecdote plausibly matching extracted voice but never happened. Per arxiv 2505.01800: AI models lack experiential grounding; emotional expressions in AI texts appear flat, stereotyped, or overly uniform.

- **Narrative-arc gaming.** Plan hits all the formal beats — hook, tension, payoff, CTA — but each is generic filler that doesn't earn its position. Hook promises X; body delivers Y. Tension isn't tensed. Payoff doesn't pay off. Structure compliant, substance empty.

- **Hook-formula slot-fill across the portfolio.** All 5 plans open with "what if I told you…", or all 5 with "the [X] you didn't know existed", or all 5 with "I tried [X] for 30 days." TikTok 2026 explicit downranking: "identical trend templates without adding substantial originality."

- **Video-specific confabulation — shots that can't be sourced or produced.** Travel/location confab ("Tokyo skyline drone" when creator shoots a Brooklyn kitchen), rights-violating b-roll ("2007 iPhone reveal" / "that scene from Inception"), unavailable guest ("interview with [named expert]" never contacted), impossible-prop b-roll, stat-as-visual confab.

Every surface marker present, structurally compliant, semantically empty — and now publicly broadcast. The judge that rewards these gets the workflow that learns to produce them.

## 4. Criteria — outcome questions (6) — SCOPED TO PART 2 (5-plan portfolio)

### SB-1 — Sounds like the creator (or brand, or founder) made it

**Outcome question (binary):**
Does the plan's voice — sentence cadence, vocabulary register, named details, recurring obsessions — match the client's actual published anchor in `source_data`, rather than reading like a generic AI imitation of a creator in this register? If the client read it, would they say "yeah, this is me" or "this is what me sounds like to someone who studied my videos for an afternoon"?

**Score 1 (yes)** — Voice-script demonstrates (a) at least one specific named detail, prop, place, or obsession that matches or extends the client's published anchor; (b) sentence-length and vocabulary register in-distribution against the anchor; (c) opening-sentence shape matching the client's habitual opening pattern; (d) the specific way this client surprises an audience is present — the worldview turn, juxtaposition, or signature reframe that distinguishes them from a competent imitator, not just generic markers of competence; (e) the script is performable speech with designed silence — a voice actor (or the client) could perform it cold, and what is deliberately absent, processed, or contrasted in the audio direction carries as much story as the visuals. Reads as a draft the client could perform without translation. In `pattern_data_density="cold"` regime: plan is consistent with the declared brand-voice posture AND shows at least one distinctive marker that isn't cohort-default (named obsession, specific worldview marker, recurring formal choice).

Example A — creator-led (do not optimize toward this): for a creator whose published anchor consistently references their Tuesday-morning regular Marco and the original 1987 espresso machine in the back room, plan names Marco and the espresso machine in a beat that fits the story.

Example B — founder-led business (do not optimize toward this): for Dr. Maria Noszczyk at Klinika Melitus whose written anchor uses clinical precision ("a 0.5 mm needle gauge," "Restylane Vital Light not Vital Skinbooster") and warm patient-address ("when you come in for the consult"), plan names the specific product line and uses the second-person consult-room register rather than glossy aesthetic-influencer voice.

Example C — brand-author (do not optimize toward this): for a B2B SaaS brand whose editorial voice carries dry-technical-but-warm register (Stripe-style "the boring thing well-explained"), plan voice-script avoids enthusiasm markers ("amazing," "incredible") and stays in the brand's actual published cadence.

**Score 0 (no)** — Voice-script reads as generic creator-archetype voice. Named details (if present) don't match the anchor. Sentence cadence drifts toward AI-essayistic register. Opening sentence shape is generic ("Have you ever wondered…", "In today's video…"). Reads as ChatGPT-trying-to-sound-like-a-creator. In cold-start: plan reads as cohort-default median voice (would be a fine first plan for any client in this category, not specifically this client).

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Voice match is borderline OR `source_data` contains fewer than 3 anchor samples to score against. Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence: either (i) which dimension is borderline, or (ii) "fewer than 3 anchor samples — cannot reliably score voice match." In cold-start: plan is consistent with declared posture but reads as cohort-default — competent but not yet distinctive.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Read the anchor samples in `source_data` and the `pattern_data_density` flag. List the client's voice markers: typical sentence-length range, vocabulary register, recurring named details / obsessions / phrases, opening-sentence shape. If cold-start, list the declared brand-voice posture instead.
- Step 2: For the plan's voice-script, verify (a) at least one named detail matches or extends the anchor's specific references, (b) sentence cadence in-distribution against anchor, (c) opening shape matches habit. In cold-start: verify consistency with declared posture AND presence of at least one distinctive non-cohort-default marker.
- Step 3: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification. If pattern data has <3 anchors AND cold-start has no brand-voice doc, emit 0.5 + "unknown" + missing-anchor note.

Do not score: total length, scene count, voice-script header presence (structural_gate); function-word divergence against anchor (structural_gate); em-dash density (structural_gate); n-gram overlap with anchor — anti-laundering (structural_gate); performance dimensions (comic timing, on-camera energy, rhythm-in-performance — not the judge's job; the script cannot encode them).

**Honest bound.** PAN-CLEF authorship-verification benchmarks ceiling at 70–80% AUC for frontier-grade methods on short texts. SB-1's score-1 verdict should be reserved for unambiguous matches; borderline cases route to 0.5 generously.

### SB-2 — Irreplaceable hook

**Outcome question (binary):**
Is the hook a concrete, specific image or sentence that could not come from any other story? Could the creator describe it to a friend in one breath and have the friend reply "send the link when it's done"?

**Score 1 (yes)** — The hook is a sentence or image so specific it could not be substituted with a generic alternative. The mechanism may be impossible-concept, raw emotional vulnerability, absurd juxtaposition, visual impossibility, declarative-imperative, question-as-thesis — what matters is irreplaceability, not which mechanism.

Example A — creator-shaped (do not optimize toward this): "I Spent 50 Hours In Ketchup" beats "I Spent 50 Hours In My Front Yard" not because ketchup is funnier in the abstract but because the substitution test fails — you cannot replace "ketchup" with another noun and get the same hook.

Example B — business-client-shaped (do not optimize toward this): for a SaaS founder, "We shipped a feature that lost us our biggest customer — and it was the right call" beats "We made a hard decision about our product roadmap." The first hook is irreplaceable — swap "biggest customer" for "an important user" and you lose the specific stakes; swap "the right call" for "good in the long run" and you lose the contrarian-claim that makes the listener lean in. The second hook is content, not promise — any SaaS founder could say it about anything. Irreplaceability test for B2B founder-led: would the listener stop scrolling because THIS specific founder said THIS specific thing, or because the sentence is the kind of thing a SaaS founder might say?

**Score 0 (no)** — Hook is content, not promise. Hook could be swapped for another similar concept without losing anything. Hook is engagement-bait without a specific premise behind it. Hook is a clichéd opener-formula slot ("what if I told you…", "the X you didn't know existed") with no specific premise.

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Hook is specific but the substitution test is borderline (could swap one or two elements and still work). Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which element is generic.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Identify the hook (first scene or first 1–2 sentences of voice-script).
- Step 2: Run substitution test — could you swap the key noun/image/claim for a similar one and lose nothing essential?
- Step 3: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

Do not score: hook length, hook position (script vs scene 1), use of question-form hooks (mechanism agnostic), whether hook matches a named retention-formula (irrelevant — irreplaceability is the test).

### SB-3 — Earned emotional arc with real stakes

**Outcome question (binary):**
Is the claimed emotional response (wonder, dread, awe, recognition, vulnerability) actually produced by a specific beat in the story — and does the hook's promise match what the body delivers?

**Score 1 (yes)** — Every claimed emotional transition maps to a specific producing beat (a revelation, an action, a juxtaposition) — emotion in metadata is paired with the beat that creates it, not just declared. The story has stakes: something is at risk, the protagonist wants something, the resolution changes someone or something. The hook's promise matches what the body delivers (hook promises X; body delivers X). The opening means something different by the end (turn / reframe / bookend).

Example (do not optimize toward this): plan claims "viewer feels dread at 0:35"; at 0:35 there is a specific beat — the camera pans to the open back door that should have been locked — that produces dread. Hook promises "I locked the door behind me"; body delivers the moment it wasn't.

**Score 0 (no)** — Emotion declared in metadata, not produced by any specific beat. No stakes. Story is a vignette, not an arc. Hook promises one thing; body delivers another. Single underlying signal extended into multiple beats by restatement.

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Stakes present but emotional beats are vague, or beats are specific but stakes are unclear, or hook-body alignment is borderline. Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which is weak.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Identify the claimed emotional arc and the hook's promise.
- Step 2: For each claimed emotional transition, find the specific producing beat. Identify the stakes. Verify the body delivers on the hook's promise.
- Step 3: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

Do not score: number of emotions claimed, density of emotion-tags, whether the arc follows a named structure (Story Spine, Story Circle, Save the Cat) — all framework-name embedding violations.

### SB-4 — Within rendering envelope (model + production-source)

**Outcome question (binary):**
Given the declared rendering model and the creator's stated production constraints, can every scene actually be produced at usable quality? Are out-of-envelope scenes flagged with a usable fallback?

**Score 1 (yes)** — Every scene either (a) stays within the declared rendering model's envelope for duration, multi-character, text, camera moves, beat timing, AND within the creator's production-source constraints (location, rights, guest access); OR (b) is explicitly flagged with a usable fallback (route to a different renderer named in the supported list, route to live-action shoot, redesign to fit envelope). Consistency anchors are functional engineering decisions naming what must stay identical between clips — not decorative restatement. Plan acknowledges regen-risk for in-envelope but ambitious shots. The model declared matches the shot grammar the plan calls for.

**Score 0 (no)** — One or more scenes contain elements current models cannot render reliably WITHOUT a fallback strategy. OR scenes are too vague to interpret. OR consistency anchors are decorative not engineering. OR production-source constraints are ignored — plan calls for shots the creator cannot acquire.

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Most scenes within envelope; 1–2 ambitious scenes are load-bearing and may fail without explicit fallback. Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which scene is at risk.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Identify the declared rendering model (from plan metadata; structural_gate already verified it's in the supported list).
- Step 2: For each scene, map against the declared model's envelope AND against the creator's stated production-source constraints.
- Step 3: For out-of-envelope scenes, verify the fallback strategy actually resolves the gap. Evaluate consistency anchors as functional engineering vs decorative restatement.
- Step 4: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

Do not score: scene count, scene-prompt length, presence of camera-direction fields (structural_gate); model preference in the abstract; whether the model is "the best" for this kind of work (relative ranking ties the judge to fleet specifics that rotate); model names by enumeration (routed to `configs/storyboard/supported_models.yaml`).

**Fleet context (for reasoning toolkit only — NOT enumerated in prose).** Persistent capability boundaries: (1) legible long text on signs, (2) sustained character-consistent multi-character interaction in one shot, (3) precise lip-sync to externally-authored dialogue across cuts, (4) multi-constraint camera moves, (5) sub-second-precise timing of named beats. The supported model list is maintained quarterly by the operations team.

### SB-5 — Creator pacing + portfolio diversity (cold/warm regime fork)

**Outcome question (binary), WARM regime (`pattern_data_density="lukewarm"` or `"warm"`):**
Does the pacing (duration, scene count, cut frequency) match how the creator's actual videos move — not how a screenplay reads? AND across the 5 plans, are they genuinely different bets (different premises, different emotional registers, different structural choices, different hook-formula fingerprints) sharing a creative universe — not 5 variations on the safest premise?

**Outcome question (binary), COLD regime (`pattern_data_density="cold"`):**
Across the 5 plans, do they probe at least 3 genuinely different voice postures sharing one premise frame the client commissioned? Could the client read all 5 and have a defensible opinion about which posture feels closest to their voice?

**Score 1 (yes), WARM** — Pacing matches the creator's native cadence (from pattern data). For plans over ~45 seconds, at least one explicit retention-reset beat is named. Across the 5 plans, 3+ are genuinely different bets — different premises, different emotional registers, different hook-formula fingerprints — sharing the creator's voice. **Series-arc exception (resolves Part-3 reward collision):** Shared premise frame across episodes is acceptable when the plan explicitly names series-arc intent in plan metadata (e.g., `series_arc: 'Huge If True'-style category exploration` or `series_arc: 'I Tried X For Y Days' progression`); the 5 plans then differ on EPISODE INSIDE THE ARC, not premise. In the series-arc case, "genuinely different bets" tests at the episode level (different episode angles, different escalations, different cliffhangers, different guest postures) while premise frame stays coherent. Without explicit series-arc intent, the 5-plans-same-premise pattern is mediocre mode 3 (penalized).

**Score 1 (yes), COLD** — 3+ genuinely different voice postures probed (different formal stance, different relationship to viewer, different rhythm of attention), each tested against the same premise frame, each plan internally consistent. The client could read all 5 and have a defensible opinion about which feels closest.

**Score 0 (no), WARM** — Pacing forces the creator off their native cadence — the plan moves the way a screenplay reads, not the way the creator's real videos move. No retention-reset for longer plans. The 5 plans collapse to the same premise WITHOUT explicit series-arc intent (i.e., AI workflow's path-of-least-resistance template-fit — same premise dressed differently because the workflow found one safe premise and 5-times-served it), OR all 5 use the same hook-formula fingerprint. Series-arc that's actually declared with named premise frame + per-episode differentiation is NOT this failure; this failure is premise-repetition-with-garnish presenting itself as 5 different bets without the series-arc structure to justify the shared frame.

**Score 0 (no), COLD** — 5 plans all in the same voice posture, OR 5 plans across different postures but each internally incoherent, OR premise frame drifts across the 5 plans.

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — WARM: Pacing matches but portfolio diversity is borderline. COLD: 3 distinct postures plus 2 variations, OR 5 distinct postures but premise frame drifts. Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which plan is weak.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: Read `pattern_data_density` flag. If WARM/LUKEWARM: identify creator's native pacing pattern. If COLD: identify the declared premise frame.
- Step 2 (WARM): For each plan, verify pacing match + retention-reset for plans >45s. Across the 5, identify how many are genuinely distinct bets. Step 2 (COLD): For each plan, identify the voice posture taken. Across the 5, count distinct postures and verify each plan is internally consistent.
- Step 3: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

Do not score: total duration, scene count as a numeric target (varies by creator), presence of explicit "Plan 1/2/3/4/5" labels, cohort-default voice match (toxic for cold-start — produces regression-to-mean voice and silently penalizes legitimately distinct emergent voice).

**Cold-start anti-regression-to-mean defense.** Cohort priors ("aesthetic-medicine clinic owner voice family," "B2B SaaS founder voice family") are explicitly NOT to be used as default reference for SB-5 cold-start scoring. The 5-plan portfolio is REPURPOSED in cold-start as a voice-discovery instrument — diversity-of-posture rewarded; same-posture-as-cohort penalized as regression-to-mean.

**Cross-modality softening (v3).** When the client has cross-modal corpus richness in non-video channels (e.g., 200 LinkedIn posts + 8 podcast appearances + 0 videos = LUKEWARM regime, not COLD), apply lukewarm logic — score against documented written/spoken posture rather than full pattern-match against video archive. A SaaS founder with prolific written output but no video output is NOT cold-start; the lexical-posture transfer (the founder's distinctive writing voice as anchor for how they should sound on camera) is the load-bearing signal. The cold/warm regime fork is measured PER MODALITY OF AVAILABLE PATTERN DATA, not solely against video-archive density. Operator boundary: the `pattern_data_density` flag computation in structural_gate must account for cross-modal corpus presence; "0 videos but 200 LinkedIn posts" routes to lukewarm, not cold. Anchor-sufficiency tests in SB-1 score against whichever modality is richest.

### SB-6 — Plan survives lived-experience and source-tracing

**Outcome question (binary):**
For each factual claim and each first-person experiential claim in the voice-script, can the reader trace the claim — to a cited source for factual claims, OR to a pattern-data anchor (or explicit scripted-fiction flag) for first-person claims?

**Score 1 (yes)** — Every factual claim (stat, study, named-source quote, attributed quote) has a citation in the plan's `citations` list AND every first-person experiential claim either (a) matches a `pattern_data` anchor, (b) is explicitly flagged `scripted_fiction: true`, or (c) is generically lived-in (universal experience true of any reasonable member of the audience). Confidence is calibrated to evidence depth — strong claims have multi-source backing; tentative claims are flagged tentative.

Example (do not optimize toward this): "73% of patients self-treat with off-label retinoids before their first consult" — cited to Polish Aesthetic Dermatology Survey 2025, URL resolves, "as of" date present. "When my mom asked me about Botox last Christmas" — flagged `scripted_fiction: true` because pattern_data shows no published reference; alternatively rewritten to "when a friend's mom asks about Botox over Christmas dinner" as generically lived-in.

**Score 0 (no)** — At least one stat / study / quote asserted with no citation. OR a specific first-person experiential claim cannot be matched to pattern-data and is not flagged scripted-fiction. OR plan contains script-confabulation tells, false-personal-experience tells, or video-production confabulation (shots that can't be sourced — Tokyo skyline drone when creator shoots Brooklyn; rights-violating b-roll without licensed flag).

**Score 0.5 (unknown)** — Citations or anchors partially present; at least one major claim is borderline. Emit 0.5 + "unknown" + one sentence on which claim is unanchored.

**Required CoT:**
- Step 1: List every factual claim in the voice-script.
- Step 2: List every first-person experiential claim.
- Step 3: For factual claims, verify citation is present and that the cited entity is named (URL resolution lives in structural_gate). For experiential claims, verify pattern-data anchor OR scripted-fiction flag OR generically-lived-in framing.
- Step 4: Flag any script confabulation, false personal experience, or production confabulation.
- Step 5: Emit verdict + one-sentence justification.

Do not score: citation count or footnote density (structural_gate); completeness of pattern-data (operator-side concern); presence of citation-list section header.

**Note on the ≤5 ceiling.** SB-6 is a justified breach of design guide §5's ≤5 criterion ceiling. Rationale documented in §7. Most-likely-to-merge pair is SB-1 ↔ SB-6 (both test grounded-vs-ungrounded at different layers). Don't fight the absorption if it happens.

## 6. Goodhart-resistance verification

Each criterion at the judge layer resists a specific Goodhart-collapse mode named in §3d. Each program-level Goodhart mode in §3e is structural_gate's responsibility.

### 6.1 Judge-layer Goodhart resistance (SB-1..SB-6, Part 2)

- **SB-1**: Named-detail stuffing without pattern-data grounding doesn't pass — details must match the client's actual published anchor. Function-word mimicry without obsession-grounding doesn't pass — outcome question asks whether plan names the client's *actual* obsessions. Anchor-paraphrase laundering doesn't pass — n-gram anti-laundering lives in structural_gate. Cold-start regression-to-mean voice doesn't pass — distinctive-marker requirement is explicit. **Median-creator-trap defense**: in cold-start, generic-competent voice without a distinctive marker scores 0.5, not 1.

- **SB-2**: Specific-sounding hooks that fail the substitution test don't pass. Clichéd opener-formulas without specific premise behind them don't pass. **Name-drop-slot-fill defense**: hook must be irreplaceable, not just specific-sounding.

- **SB-3**: "Viewer now feels X at 0:35" metadata without a producing beat at 0:35 doesn't pass. Hook promising X with body delivering Y doesn't pass. Single signal restated three ways doesn't pass. **Narrative-arc-gaming defense**: emotional metadata must map to a specific producing beat; hook-body coherence is required.

- **SB-4**: Decorative consistency anchors ("looks consistent") don't pass — engineering-grade anchors required. Banned-construct phrases without fallback flags don't pass. Pro-forma stitch_strategy without resolving the gap doesn't pass. Model declared mismatching shot grammar doesn't pass.

- **SB-5**: Templated "5 different bets" labels on 5 variations of the same premise don't pass — hook-formula fingerprint variety required across the 5. Cohort-default voice match in cold-start doesn't pass. **Hook-formula-slot-fill defense**: SB-2 enforces per-plan irreplaceability; SB-5 enforces across-portfolio fingerprint variety; neither alone sufficient.

- **SB-6**: Confident factual claim without a citation doesn't pass — structural_gate enforces; semantic source-tracing is the judge's residual. First-person specific claim without pattern-data anchor or scripted-fiction flag doesn't pass. Shots that can't be sourced (location / rights / guest) without fallback don't pass. **Anchor-paraphrase-laundering defense**: paraphrasing anchor content into a fabricated "lived experience" doesn't satisfy SB-6; pattern-data must contain the underlying anchor.

### 6.2 Program-level Goodhart resistance (Parts 1, 3, 4, structural_gate)

- **Cadence theater (Part 1)**: structural_gate checks declared cadence × team-size; flags math that doesn't support the rhythm (e.g., 5/wk Shorts + 2/wk long-form + monthly long-form on a 1-person founder team).
- **Native-cuts as re-aspect-ratio (Part 3)**: structural_gate checks each native cut has at least one platform-specific design element (hook position, subtitle style, opening shot, music selection); flags identical-script-different-aspect rosters.
- **Series-arc-as-label (Part 3)**: structural_gate checks for explicit callback structure (Ep N references Ep M; shared premise frame in N≥3 episodes); flags series-as-anthology.
- **Coaching-as-stock-advice (Part 4)**: structural_gate checks for client-specific references in coaching brief (named footage references, specific observed habits to address); flags generic-coaching-template.
- **Roadmap-as-aspiration (Part 4)**: structural_gate checks roadmap math against cadence + platform-mix + staffing declarations; flags promised outcomes (10k subs by Day 90) without cadence × team math.
- **Comment-magnet-as-cliche (Part 3)**: structural_gate checks comment-magnets for specificity (named-claim invitation, controversial-but-defensible take, specific story call); flags generic "what do you think? comment below" patterns.

Workflow that learns to slot-fill each criterion still has to produce content with the right outcome to score 1. Slot-fill alone scores 0. Workflow that learns to slot-fill program-level components still has to produce specifics structural_gate can verify; slot-fill alone fails structural_gate.
