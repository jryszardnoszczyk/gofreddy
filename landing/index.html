§ 05 · known_objections

Answered in advance.

01   why not just hire an agency? ›
Agencies bill the org chart, not the output. You pay a director you never see, an account manager whose job is to forward emails, and a junior who does the actual work. I remove everyone except the work. Same deliverables, 3–6× cheaper, hours-not-weeks iteration.
02   can one person really replace a marketing team? ›
Depends on what the team does. Content research, competitive monitoring, publishing, reporting — yes, those. Buyer interviews, brand strategy, live events — no. The audit tells you which kind of team you have.
03   why so cheap? what's the catch? ›
No catch. No 15 salaries, no rented office, no sales team. You pay for output. Your ceiling is my calendar — which is why I cap at six operate clients.
04   bus factor — what if you disappear? ›
You own the code. Every build ships with runbooks, deployment keys in your name, and a README a mid-level engineer can read. If I vanish tomorrow, your distribution keeps running. Try getting that out of an agency contract.
05   isn't this just chatgpt with extra steps? ›
ChatGPT is an ingredient. Freddy is the kitchen. Real distribution systems need data ingress, scoring, routing, cost control, audit trails, error handling, secret management, and someone on call when a provider changes their API. A chatbot has none of that.
06   what if the audit finds nothing? ›
If the audit doesn't name at least three specific distribution gaps and a plan to close them, you get a refund. Haven't had to issue one.
07   do I have to continue after the audit? ›
No. The audit is complete on its own. You get the report, the raw data, and full ownership. Build and operate are separate decisions made with more information.
