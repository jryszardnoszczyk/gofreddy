§ 01 · What I build

Three pipelines.
One engine.

Every Freddy build is the same architecture in different shapes. Three autonomous systems that read, decide, and ship — driven by the same audit and the same operator.

Pipeline 01

Content engines.

Autonomous content for X, LinkedIn, blog, email. Keyword + competitor mining → angle generation → drafts → AI judges → publish. Audit trail on every decision.

Outputs: daily-to-weekly cadence, platform-native formatting, ranked drafts ready for human review.
Pipeline 02

Competitive intelligence.

Continuous scan of competitors, mentions, market shifts. Ad libraries, news, social, search results, reddit, SEO movement. Surfaced as ranked signals you can act on — not a dashboard of noise.

Outputs: weekly digest, real-time alerts on competitor launches, monthly market briefs.
Pipeline 03

Distribution + reporting.

Multi-platform publishing with native formatting. Scheduled, tracked, A/B'd. Performance tied back to specific bets — so every report names what worked and what didn't.

Outputs: publishing cadence, monthly performance reports, cost-per-output ledger.

Each pipeline is its own scope. Audit decides which you need — usually two of three, sometimes all three.

§ 05 · Built for

Three categories.
Same architecture, different shapes.

Distribution looks different depending on who's hunting whom — engineer-buyers, judgment-buyers, regulated audiences. The engine doesn't change. The pipelines on it do.

B2B SaaS / Enterprise

Series-A to C founders.

Technical-buyer audiences who research before they ever talk to sales. Long-form engineering content, comparison pieces, technical case studies, search-rank defense, share-of-voice tracking, competitor launch alerts.

Typical mix: content engine + competitive intelligence. Skip distribution if you already have a strong publishing rhythm.
Specialty healthcare

Clinics + practices.

Regulated audiences hunting for trust signals. Local-SEO defense, treatment-information content, review-platform monitoring, patient-question mining from forums. Compliance-aware by default.

Live example: dermatology clinic in Europe — patient-facing content + local SERP + review intelligence.
Professional services

Boutique firms.

Judgment-buyers hiring on perceived expertise. Thought-leadership content engines, regulation and ruling monitoring, competitor partner-move tracking. Often paired with a quarterly market briefing.

Live example: boutique legal firm in Europe — authority content + ruling digest + competitor moves.

Outside these three? The audit decides if there's a fit. Categories aren't gates — they're the shapes that have shipped.

§ 07 · Known objections

Answered in advance.

01   what does freddy actually do? ›
Audit your distribution surface, then build and run the autonomous systems your marketing team would build — content pipelines, publishing, monitoring, competitive intelligence. Engineering work, not campaign work. You own the source, the keys, the data. One operator plus agents replaces a team of ten operating the old way.
02   why not just hire an agency? ›
Agencies bill the org chart, not the output. You pay a director you never see, an account manager whose job is to forward emails, and a junior who does the actual work. I remove everyone except the work. Same deliverables, 3–6× cheaper, hours-not-weeks iteration.
03   can one person really replace a marketing team? ›
Depends on what the team does. Content research, competitive monitoring, publishing, reporting — yes, those. Buyer interviews, brand strategy, live events — no. The audit tells you which kind of team you have.
04   why so cheap? what's the catch? ›
No catch. No 15 salaries, no rented office, no sales team. You pay for output. Your ceiling is my calendar — which is why I cap at six operate clients.
05   bus factor — what if you disappear? ›
You own the code. Every build ships with runbooks, deployment keys in your name, and a README a mid-level engineer can read. If I vanish tomorrow, your distribution keeps running. Try getting that out of an agency contract.
06   isn't this just chatgpt with extra steps? ›
ChatGPT is an ingredient. Freddy is the kitchen. Real distribution systems need data ingress, scoring, routing, cost control, audit trails, error handling, secret management, and someone on call when a provider changes their API. A chatbot has none of that.
07   what if the audit finds nothing? ›
If the audit doesn't name at least three specific distribution gaps and a plan to close them, you get a refund. Haven't had to issue one.
08   do I have to continue after the audit? ›
No. The audit is complete on its own. You get the report, the raw data, and full ownership. Operate is a separate decision, made with more information once the audit is in.
